## Risks

### 1. Pepe IP Complexity
**Risk:** Pepe the Frog is Matt Furie's IP, associated with political movements. PiPi draws from the Pepe aesthetic lineage.
**Mitigation:** PiPi is distinct — a pig, not a frog. Pink, not green. The "Pepe" in the name is cultural shorthand (same lineage, different character). PiPi is explicitly joyful and constructive — antithetical to the nihilism Pepe has been associated with. Legal review before large-scale NFT launch.

### 2. Brand Dilution
**Risk:** PiPi posted everywhere, used by everyone, loses distinctiveness. Becomes background noise.
**Mitigation:** Voice guidelines strictly maintained. Quality over quantity after Phase 1. Community creator program with curation. PiPi is a character with consistent values — content that breaks voice gets corrected.

### 3. Cultural Appropriation Backlash
**Risk:** Meme culture communities react poorly to "corporate" or "mission-driven" characters trying to be authentic.
**Mitigation:** PiPi's authenticity is genuine — the character's values are the ecosystem's actual values. PiPi posts must always feel like they come from a real character, not a marketing team. Otto must internalize the voice, not perform it.

### 4. Meme Lifecycle
**Risk:** Memes have lifecycles. PiPi could peak early and fade before ecosystem utility is live.
**Mitigation:** PiPi is a character, not a meme format. Characters outlast memes (Pepe himself is 20 years old). Depth of lore, consistent voice, and expanding utility create longevity that trend memes lack.

### 5. Polkadot Pitch Rejection
**Risk:** Polkadot Foundation declines or ignores the mascot pitch.
**Mitigation:** PiPi succeeds with or without Polkadot. The pitch is upside, not dependency. If rejected, PiPi continues as MY3YE's cultural engine. If accepted, PiPi gains massive legitimacy and resources.
