## The Mission

**505 Systems is two things fused into one organism:**

### Mission 1 — The Self-Education System
Takes anyone from zero to skilled using their own strengths and passions. The fastest path to advancement is contributing to the most critical parts of the system. Universal curriculum. No prerequisites. Contribution-based advancement.

**The principle:** You don't prove you're ready to contribute. Contributing is how you prove you're ready for the next level.

### Mission 2 — The Refuge & Extraction System
For people fleeing war zones, living in underprivileged areas, or experiencing homelessness. A real path forward — not charity, not a handout. Arrive from anywhere. Learn by contributing what matters most. Rise through effort and impact. Works physically alongside Tusita locations.

**The principle:** The crisis is an entry point, not a disqualifier.

### The Unified Ladder
These two missions are one system:

```
ARRIVAL ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────► IMPACT
  │                                                                │
  ▼                                                                ▼
Anyone, anywhere               →  Learn by contributing  →  Rise by earning
War zone, homeless, curious,      High-priority system         DPC weight, skills,
broke, underskilled, lost         needs get taught first        governance voice,
                                                                   livelihood
```

**The governance backbone** makes this trustworthy: every advancement, every contribution, every decision — recorded on-chain, transparent, auditable, not subject to favoritism.

## The DPC Model — Full Specification

Governance weight is earned, not bought. Not one token, one vote. One contribution, proportional weight.

### Three Factors

| Factor | Definition | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Structural Impact** | Did your contribution change the architecture? | Core code merged, governance proposal ratified, protocol-level design |
| **Consistent Energy** | Sustained engagement over time, not burst contributions | Monthly contributions > one-time spikes; streak multiplier |
| **Weighted Resonance** | Alignment with mission, verified by peers + on-chain outcomes | Peer endorsements, on-chain impact metrics, outcome tracking |

### Capital Amplifier
- Token stake amplifies DPC weight up to **3×** (not 10×, not unlimited)
- Prevents pure plutocracy (capital alone can't dominate)
- Base DPC still comes from participation — capital amplifies, does not replace

### Contribution Types & Weights (v0.1)
| Type | Base DPC |
|---|---|
| Code merged to critical system | High |
| Governance proposal ratified | High |
| Teaching / onboarding verified new contributor | Medium-High |
| Verified content for ecosystem | Medium |
| Hosting / stewarding at Tusita | Medium |
| Completing a learning pathway | Low-Medium |
| Refuge intake coordination | Medium-High (hardest work) |
| Financial contribution | Variable (capital amplifier) |

### Decay + Compounding
- DPC **decays without activity** (prevents dormant whale governance)
- DPC **compounds with sustained contribution** (rewards consistent builders)
- Decay rate: ~5% per month of inactivity (suggested; governance can amend)

## Risks

### 1. Governance Capture
- **Risk:** Whale coalition acquires enough stake to overwhelm DPC base weight
- **Mitigation:** 3× cap on capital amplifier; contribution-based DPC cannot be purchased; constitutional quorum requires broad participation minimum
- **Signal:** Watch for wallet concentration in treasury + voting power

### 2. Scope Creep
- **Risk:** Education + refuge + governance = three large systems; doing all at once means doing none well
- **Mitigation:** Phase 1 is design only. Phase 2 delivers education MVP. Phase 3 delivers refuge intake. Governance infrastructure first — it enables everything else. Don't build the ladder before the ground floor is solid.
- **Rule:** Nothing ships without a governance vote approving it (even internally — this enforces the priority discipline)

### 3. Underfunding Critical Infrastructure
- **Risk:** Refuge intake has real physical costs (food, shelter, coordination staff). DAO treasury may not fund it adequately, or community priorities may drift toward exciting technical work over unglamorous intake ops.
- **Mitigation:** Constitutional earmark — minimum % of treasury allocated to refuge intake permanently (cannot be reduced without supermajority + Sovereign review)
- **Backup:** Tusita revenue model covers intake costs at first location (refuge intake is subsidized by travel/tourism income)

### 4. Education System Quality
- **Risk:** Contribution-as-curriculum works only if contributions are genuinely valuable. Gaming the system (worthless contributions earning DPC) degrades both the credentials and the governance.
- **Mitigation:** Steward review of all contribution claims. On-chain outcome tracking (did the contribution actually improve the system?). DPC fraud detection via anomaly detection (Otto AI)

### 5. Trauma-Informed Design
- **Risk:** Refuge intake serving people in genuine crisis requires more than a form and a pathway. Poor intake design causes harm.
- **Mitigation:** Design with advisors who have direct refugee support experience. Pilot with small cohort before scaling. Tusita Stewards trained in trauma-informed facilitation.

## The Anti-Dystopia Design

Most decentralized projects fail because governance becomes one of:
1. **Plutocratic** — rich whales win every vote; community theater
2. **Abandoned** — no one votes; founding team decides everything anyway
3. **Captured** — one faction gains control and entrenches itself

505 Systems solves all three:
- **DPC prevents plutocracy** — contribution weight, not capital weight (capital amplifies, does not replace)
- **Contribution-as-curriculum prevents abandonment** — people have personal advancement incentives to stay engaged
- **Constitutional constraints prevent capture** — supermajority + quorum requirements; no single wallet or coalition can dominate

**The unified ladder is the engine:** when rising through the system requires actually contributing to it, governance participation is inherently aligned with building the mission — not extracting from it.
