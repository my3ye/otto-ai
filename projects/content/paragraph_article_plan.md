## Core Strategy: Progressive Reveal Logic

**The principle:** Don't reveal everything at once. Each article should answer one question and open three more. The reader who finishes Article 1 should feel like they've just seen the tip of something enormous.

**The gate model:**
- **Gate 1** (Articles 1–3): WHY — the structural problem. Hook the thinker.
- **Gate 2** (Articles 4–8): WHAT — the infrastructure layer. Earn the builder.
- **Gate 3** (Articles 9–12): HOW IT LIVES — the life & culture layer. Win the community.
- **Gate 4** (Articles 13–16): THE WHOLE PICTURE — scale and economics. Convert the investor.
- **Gate 5** (Articles 17+): CONVERGENCE — show how all pieces interlock. Reveal the civilization.

**Each article ends with:**
1. A single sentence that summarizes the piece
2. A tease of the next article ("next: what happens when [X] becomes infrastructure")
3. A call to action (follow, collect, share — never all three, pick one)

## Key Principles for ALL Paragraph Articles

1. **Never beg.** Don't ask people to share, like, subscribe in the first sentence. The work earns attention.
2. **One idea per article.** One project, one argument, one door opened.
3. **End with a door, not a wall.** Every ending points toward the next piece.
4. **Use the voice:** Confident, not preachy. Clear, not dumbed down. Short sentences. No corporate hedging.
5. **Always link the inception article.** Every project article links to its inception article on my3ye.xyz.
6. **Collect don't just read.** Encourage collecting on Paragraph (if monetized) — it funds the work and creates a record of who was early.
7. **The river metaphor.** When in doubt: MY3YE is the river. Every project is a lake. The water runs through all of them.
