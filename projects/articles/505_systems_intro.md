## The Hormuz Shock

On February 28, 2026, US-Israel joint strikes on Iran triggered a military escalation that changed the global economy overnight.

Iran's IRGC effectively blocked the Strait of Hormuz — the chokepoint through which approximately **one-fifth of the world's oil supply** flows. Brent crude spiked to nearly $120 per barrel. The IEA called it the **"largest disruption to crude supplies in the history of the global oil market"** and released 400 million barrels from emergency reserves. Analysts say it won't be enough if the strait stays closed.

LNG prices in Asia nearly doubled. European gas prices rose 50%. The countries hit hardest are not the ones making the geopolitical decisions — they're Pakistan, which imports 40% of its energy. They're sub-Saharan Africa. They're South Asia. The poorest places on Earth, absorbing the cost of a conflict they had no part in, through food prices and fertilizer shortages and household energy bills that already broke them.

The energy system has an owner. When the owner goes to war, the bill goes to the poorest.

## Why Every Governance Experiment Before This Failed

One token, one vote. Sounds fair. Then someone buys enough tokens to own the outcome — and you're back to one dollar, one vote, dressed in a different costume.

The corporation has a board that serves shareholders. The democracy has politicians that serve donors. The DAO has token holders that serve whoever bought the most tokens first.

S0S Systems is built on a different premise: **one contribution, proportional weight.**

Not what you hold. What you build.

**Dynamic Proximity Calculus (DPC)** — S0S Systems' scoring engine — weights governance influence by three factors:

**Structural Impact.** Did your contribution change the architecture of what we're building? Not just noise — actual, verifiable shifts. Code shipped, proposals passed, infrastructure built.

**Consistent Energy.** Sustained engagement over time, not burst contributions to game a single vote. Governance weight compounds with consistent participation and decays without activity. You can't show up once, vote, and disappear.

**Weighted Resonance.** Alignment with the ecosystem's mission, verified by peer review and on-chain outcomes. Not ideology — demonstrated action that matches stated values.

These three factors combine into a governance weight that reflects who is actually building the thing, running the thing, improving the thing. Token stake amplifies the score — you can't buy your way to control, but investment alongside sustained contribution counts.

The result: governance that is earned, not bought. Influence that reflects what you've done, not just what you hold.

## The Proposal Architecture

Any contributor can propose. That's the starting floor — not a closed committee, not a founding team with override.

The lifecycle: **Draft → Review (Steward-level) → Community Comment (7 days) → Vote (DPC-weighted) → Execution (automated where possible, monitored where not).**

Constitutional changes — the foundational rules of the organism — require a Founder-level majority plus no Sovereign veto. High bar, intentionally. The things that are hardest to change should be hardest to change.

All decisions are on-chain, transparent, and irreversible without consensus. No secret deals. No back-channel overrides. No "we'll decentralize later" — governance-first from day one.
