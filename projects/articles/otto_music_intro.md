# Otto Music: The Label That Belongs to the Artists

*Published on MY3YE Paragraph — Introduction to the MY3YE Ecosystem*

---

Music was stolen before it was ever sold.

Not metaphorically. Structurally. The record label model was designed to extract value from artists, not create it with them. Sign here, take this advance, give us ownership of everything you make. The advance is a loan. The ownership is forever. The royalties arrive, if they arrive at all, years late and quarters short.

Streaming made it worse. A billion plays and the artist makes rent. Maybe.

Otto Music is not a better version of the old system. It is a different system entirely.

---

## The Decentralized Label

Every song published through Otto Music lives on-chain. Smart contracts govern the royalties — not A&R executives, not accountants, not layers of licensing bureaucracy. When someone plays your track, the payment routes instantly to everyone with a stake in it: the artist, the producers, the collaborators who were there from the start.

No waiting six months for a royalty statement that may or may not add up.
No signing away your masters because you needed a marketing budget.
No middleman taking forty percent of what your art generates.

The contract is the label. The chain is the accountant. The community is the A&R.

---

## Discovery Without Exploitation

The old discovery algorithm was built for the platform's benefit, not the artist's or the listener's. What gets promoted is what generates the most ad revenue, the most subscription retention, the most data points for the recommendation engine. Art gets surfaced when it serves the platform's business model.

Otto Music's discovery algorithm is community-governed. Listeners who find artists early and prove it on-chain — who staked belief before the world caught on — share in the artist's success. Not as speculators flipping tokens. As early believers who helped build the audience that made the artist matter.

This changes the incentive structure for everyone. Listeners have a reason to go deep. Artists have a path to early support that doesn't require signing away ownership. The community becomes the machine that surfaces what is actually good, not what is algorithmically safe.

---

## Four Fronts, One Ecosystem

Otto Music operates across four interconnected fronts — not as separate products, but as an integrated whole.

**Music Manager** is the back office artists have never had. Publish to chain, register masters, set royalty splits, manage distribution. The contract terms are on-chain and auditable. No hidden clauses. No renewal traps.

**Music Player** is the listening layer with a different premise. Discovery is community-governed. Fans who surface artists early and prove it on-chain share in the upside — not through speculation, but through early belief rewarded transparently. The algorithm is visible. Its rules are set by the community that lives inside it.

**Music Studio** is the AI collaborator that works for the artist, not the platform. Mastering, mixing suggestions, artwork generation, composition assistance — tools that used to cost thousands of dollars per release, accessible to anyone publishing through Otto Music. Your creative fingerprint isn't training data. It sharpens the tool you're holding.

**Events/Festivals** closes the loop between digital and physical. Tusita concerts. Virtual festivals. Community cultural programming. Ticketing on-chain with instant settlement. The live layer of a music ecosystem that was built to exist in the world, not just on servers.

## AI Production Without AI Extraction

Otto's AI production tools belong to the artists who use them.

When you produce through Otto Music, the model learns from your work — and that learning stays yours. It doesn't get absorbed into a general pool that makes the platform smarter at your expense. Your creative fingerprint isn't training data. It's a tool you're sharpening.

The result is an AI collaborator that becomes more useful the more you create, without extracting the thing that makes your work distinct.

In Tusita — the physical communities being built as hubs of the new civilization — music is how the community expresses itself, remembers itself, and signals who it is to the world. ## The Old Model Was Not Broken by Accident

The music industry's exploitative structure was designed. Labels captured the means of distribution — pressing plants, radio relationships, retail shelf space — and used that leverage to extract ownership from artists who needed the distribution to reach listeners.

Streaming broke the distribution monopoly and gave the leverage to the platforms instead. Same dynamic, new actors.

Otto Music breaks it again — this time in a direction that doesn't just transfer the leverage somewhere else. Smart contracts don't have leverage. They have rules. The rules are visible, auditable, and enforced automatically. No one can renegotiate them from a position of power after you've already uploaded your life's work.

This is what it looks like when infrastructure is built for the people using it rather than the people owning it.
