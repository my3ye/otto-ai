# Otto Devices: Hardware Built for Sovereignty, Not Surveillance

*Published on MY3YE Paragraph — Introduction to the MY3YE Ecosystem*

---

Every device you carry is built around a business model.

The phone tracks your location because that data has value to the people who built it. The fitness band monitors your heart because your health profile is worth more to advertisers and insurers than it is inconvenient to collect. The smart speaker listens because ambient data is an asset. The laptop connects to a cloud because keeping your data local would reduce your dependence on the platform.

Your devices are not tools you own. They are portals through which corporations continuously extract something from you in exchange for access to the hardware.

This is not an accident of design. It is the design.

Otto Devices is built on the opposite premise.

---

## The Principle

Hardware should serve the person holding it. Not the company that manufactured it. Not the cloud it connects to. Not the advertising network that monetizes the behavioral data it generates.

Every Otto device is designed from first principles with sovereignty as the baseline requirement — not a premium feature, not a marketing claim, not a setting buried in the privacy menu. The architecture starts with: what does the person using this device need, and how do we build the hardware to serve that need without creating extraction pathways?

Then we build it.

## The Line

**Otto Puck.** Your node in the network. Portable compute designed for connectivity without dependence on centralized infrastructure. The Puck enables you to participate in the ONEON sovereign network from anywhere — a physical anchor for digital independence. Carry it like a key. Treat it like infrastructure.

**Otto Phone.** A phone that earns your trust by not surveilling you to fund its existence. Hardware-level privacy architecture. Built on ONEON's communications layer. Your calls, messages, and location are not the product. You are not the product. The phone is the product, and it works for you.

**Otto Band.** The biometric wearable that treats your health data as yours. Heart rate, sleep, stress, recovery — continuous biometric monitoring that feeds Shakrah's wellness infrastructure on terms you control. No corporate partner receives your health profile. No insurance algorithm scores you. The Band generates data that belongs to the person wearing it, and the architecture enforces this, not just the privacy policy.

**Otto Buds.** Sensory augmentation for the builders who need to stay sharp. Real-time translation across language boundaries. Spatial audio that doesn't isolate you from your environment. Environmental awareness that enhances rather than replaces your own perception. AI in your ear that works for you, not for the platform.

**Otto Home.** Residential intelligence that runs locally. Energy management, home security, communications, environmental monitoring — orchestrated by a node that lives in your home, not in a data center you can't visit and a corporation you can't audit. Smart home functionality that doesn't require trusting a third party with the sensor data of your private life.

**Otto Tower.** The community anchor. Every Tusita settlement deploys a Tower — shared compute, communications hub, community network node. The Tower makes the community self-sufficient: its own connectivity, its own redundancy, its own foundation for the digital infrastructure the community depends on. It is the backbone of the local network, owned collectively, governed by the people it serves.

**Otto Satellites.** The long arc. Collectively-owned orbital compute that completes ONEON's network independence — sovereign internet access from orbit, without routing through infrastructure controlled by governments or corporations that can audit, throttle, or terminate the connection. The satellites are the endpoint of physical network sovereignty.

## Purpose-Scaled Intelligence

Every device in the Otto line carries an AI chip calibrated to its role. Not general-purpose compute forced into consumer-electronics packaging. Intelligence matched to function: the Band's chip processes biometric data efficiently without overpowering the battery budget; the Tower's chip handles community-scale coordination; the Puck's chip enables network participation without requiring a data center in your pocket.

The chips are designed in-house. The purpose shapes the silicon. This is what it looks like when hardware is designed for a function rather than for a bill of materials.

## Built by Ottolabs, Governed by the Community

Otto Devices is the product line of Ottolabs — the physical manufacturing and R&D arm of the MY3YE ecosystem. The factories that produce Otto devices are collective infrastructure: owned by the community, operated for the mission, not for shareholder returns.

Device updates are community-governed. Roadmap priorities are shaped by the people using the hardware, not a product team optimizing engagement metrics. When the community identifies a privacy vulnerability, the fix isn't delayed for a quarterly release cycle — the organism responds.

This is the practical consequence of building hardware inside a DAO. The governance isn't decorative. It reaches into how the physical objects are designed, updated, and improved.
