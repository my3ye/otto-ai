# Ottolabs: The Means of Production Belong to the Many

*Published on MY3YE Paragraph — Introduction to the MY3YE Ecosystem*

---

Every revolution in human organization has eventually hit the same wall.

The ideas were right. The governance was right. The community was committed. But the factories still belonged to someone else. The infrastructure still ran on someone else's hardware. The food still came from someone else's supply chain. And whoever controlled the physical layer controlled everything above it, regardless of how elegant the software was.

Ottolabs is the answer to that wall.

---

## What Ottolabs Builds

Ottolabs is the physical manufacturing and R&D arm of the MY3YE ecosystem. It builds the tangible layer that everything else runs on: the devices, the robots, the energy systems, the factories, the farms.

Not as a corporation with shareholders extracting returns. As collective infrastructure — owned by the community that depends on it, governed by contribution, maintained for the mission rather than for quarterly earnings.

The question Ottolabs answers is simple and hard: what does it actually take to build a self-sufficient civilization?

The answer is: everything we're building.

## The Device Ecosystem

The Otto device line is purpose-scaled intelligence, from personal to planetary.

**Otto Puck.** Portable compute. Sovereign internet access without dependence on centralized ISPs or surveillance-ready hardware. The Puck is your node in the network — small enough to carry, powerful enough to participate.

**Otto Phone.** A phone that doesn't surveil you to fund its business model. Hardware-level privacy. Runs on ONEON's sovereign network. Your communications belong to you.

**Otto Band.** The biometric wearable that doesn't sell your health data. Every pulse, every sleep cycle, every stress marker — that data is yours, encrypted, owned by the wearer. Shakrah uses it. No corporation gets it without your explicit consent.

**Otto Buds.** Sensory AI that augments without replacing. Spatial audio, real-time translation, environmental awareness. Built for the builders who need to stay sharp.

**Otto Home.** The residential intelligence node. Energy management, security, communications — running locally, not through a corporate cloud that can be audited, subpoenaed, or shut down by a government request.

**Otto Tower.** The community anchor. Every Tusita settlement gets a Tower — the shared physical compute and communications hub that makes the community node self-sufficient and connected to the broader network.

**Otto Satellites.** Long-term: collectively-owned orbital compute. Sovereign internet from orbit. The mesh that completes ONEON's network independence.

## Robotics at Every Scale

Physical sovereignty requires physical capability. Ottolabs' robotics program builds across four tiers:

**Humanoid tier.** Capable general-purpose robots that can work alongside humans on complex tasks in Tusita communities and manufacturing environments.

**Industrial tier.** Heavy automation for Ottolabs' own factories. Machines that build the machines. Production capacity that scales without proportionally scaling labor costs.

**Agricultural tier.** Food sovereignty is real sovereignty. Agricultural robots that enable Tusita communities to feed themselves — precision farming at the scale of a settlement, not a corporation.

**Micro-swarm tier.** Distributed autonomous systems for environmental monitoring, precision maintenance, and tasks that require coordination across many small agents rather than a few large ones.

This isn't a distant vision. It's an engineering roadmap. The sequence matters — each tier funds and enables the next.

## Energy and Manufacturing

A sovereign community that depends on external energy supply is not sovereign. Ottolabs builds renewable energy infrastructure for Tusita communities — solar, storage, and distribution designed to be owned and operated by the community rather than leased from a utility.

The manufacturing facilities follow the same logic. Ottolabs builds its own factories — initially for Otto devices, ultimately as general production capacity that the community owns. The factories produce what the community needs. Surplus production becomes economic output that funds the next phase.
