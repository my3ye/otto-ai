## 3. TOP 10 IMMEDIATE TARGETS — WEB3 ECOSYSTEM

**Target profile:** Web3-native startup, Seed to Series A, $500K–$10M raised, likely has a weak or non-existent marketing website.  
**Why they need WebAssist:** Crypto founders build product first, website second — or never. Discord-first teams often have no real web presence despite having strong communities and real funding.

---

### T1 — DePIN Protocol (Infrastructure)
- **Profile type:** Decentralized physical infrastructure network. Raised $2–5M. Chain: Solana or IoTeX.
- **Website reality:** Usually a whitepaper landing page. No SEO. No conversion flow. No blog.
- **Why WebAssist:** DePIN requires enterprise and hardware partner credibility. A whitepaper-only site loses those conversations.
- **Where to find them:** Messari DePIN reports, DePIN Summit attendee lists, Solana Ecosystem page
- **Pitch angle:** "Your hardware partners will Google you. What do they find?"

### T2 — Web3 Gaming Studio (Gaming/NFT)
- **Profile type:** Indie Web3 game studio, 8–20 person team. Raised $1–3M. Has a Discord, a trailer, and a broken website.
- **Website reality:** Often a Squarespace or Wix site with outdated screenshots. Roadmap not reflected.
- **Why WebAssist:** Gamers research before minting. Press checks websites before coverage. Both need a credible home.
- **Where to find them:** Fractal, GameFi ecosystem trackers, GamesBeat coverage of Web3 gaming
- **Pitch angle:** "Your trailer is good. Your website is killing the follow-through."

### T3 — DAO Tooling Company (Governance)
- **Profile type:** Building governance or treasury tooling for DAOs. Raised $1–5M. Very technical, design-light.
- **Website reality:** GitHub README-level site. Docs are great. Marketing is nonexistent.
- **Why WebAssist:** DAO adoption requires decision-maker buy-in — treasuries, multisigs, governance leads. They need a site that explains the product to non-technical stakeholders.
- **Where to find them:** Tally ecosystem, Snapshot integrations, DAOhaus community, OpenZeppelin Forum
- **Pitch angle:** "Technical teams buy with docs. Non-technical budget holders buy with websites."

### T4 — NFT Infrastructure Startup (Infrastructure)
- **Profile type:** Building minting tools, royalty infrastructure, or NFT analytics. Raised $1–3M. Post-hype cycle, now focused on B2B enterprise.
- **Website reality:** Built for 2021 hype, never updated. Looks like a previous crypto cycle.
- **Why WebAssist:** B2B pivot requires trust signals a 2021-era site can't provide.
- **Where to find them:** NFT infrastructure roundups, Manifold community, Reservoir Protocol ecosystem
- **Pitch angle:** "Your product evolved past 2021. Your website didn't."

### T5 — Cross-Chain Bridge or Interoperability Protocol (Infrastructure)
- **Profile type:** Technical team building bridging or interoperability. Often $3–8M raised. Heavy on developers, light on marketing.
- **Website reality:** Developer documentation is excellent. The marketing homepage has 3 sentences and a "learn more" button that goes nowhere.
- **Why WebAssist:** Enterprise integrations and VC follow-on rounds require a real web presence that speaks to non-technical partners.
- **Where to find them:** LayerZero ecosystem grants, Axelar grant recipients, Wormhole partner directory
- **Pitch angle:** "Your protocol documentation is outstanding. Your homepage is not."

### T6 — Web3 Analytics / Data Tool (Data)
- **Profile type:** On-chain analytics or wallet intelligence startup. Raised $500K–$2M. Small team, product-first.
- **Website reality:** Minimal landing page. No case studies. No content. No SEO.
- **Why WebAssist:** Analytics buyers (funds, protocols, enterprises) research alternatives online before committing. No website = no discovery.
- **Where to find them:** Dune Analytics ecosystem, Nansen competitor tracking, DeFiLlama tool directory
- **Pitch angle:** "Funds discover tools through Google before Telegram. Are you showing up?"

### T7 — Crypto Wallet / Key Management Startup (Consumer/Security)
- **Profile type:** Building a non-custodial wallet or key management layer. Raised $1–4M. App-first, website-neglected.
- **Website reality:** App Store listing + a minimal site with a download button. No education, no differentiation, no SEO.
- **Why WebAssist:** Wallet users research trust and security before installing. A weak website undercuts the trust the product is selling.
- **Where to find them:** EF ESP grant recipients, Gitcoin wallet projects, Web3 security-focused VCs' portfolios
- **Pitch angle:** "You're selling trust. Your website needs to sell it first."

### T8 — Web3 Developer Tooling (Developer Infrastructure)
- **Profile type:** Building SDKs, RPC infrastructure, or smart contract tooling. Raised $500K–$3M. Docs-first culture.
- **Website reality:** Docs are excellent, homepage is neglected — same Docusaurus template as 40 other projects.
- **Why WebAssist:** Enterprise developer teams evaluate tooling by website quality as a proxy for team quality. Docs don't close enterprise deals — the marketing site does.
- **Where to find them:** Alchemy developer community, QuickNode ecosystem, Hardhat integrations, thirdweb partner list
- **Pitch angle:** "Enterprise devs evaluate you before they try your SDK. What do they see?"

### T9 — Tokenized Real-World Assets (RWA) Startup (DeFi/TradFi Bridge)
- **Profile type:** Bringing real estate, credit, or commodities on-chain. Raised $2–8M. Targets institutional and retail investors.
- **Website reality:** Often a pitch-deck-turned-website. No live copy. No SEO. Legal disclaimers everywhere.
- **Why WebAssist:** RWA requires legitimacy that only a polished web presence provides. Both retail and institutional audience segments need to trust before investing.
- **Where to find them:** Centrifuge ecosystem, Ondo Finance partner network, RWA.xyz directory
- **Pitch angle:** "You're asking people to trust you with real money. Your website is the first proof point."

### T10 — Web3 Social / Creator Platform (Consumer)
- **Profile type:** Decentralized social, creator monetization, or identity platform. Raised $1–5M. Strong community, weak marketing site.
- **Website reality:** Usually a beautiful app but a neglected dot-com. Site doesn't explain the product to people who haven't heard of it.
- **Why WebAssist:** Growth depends on non-crypto-native users discovering the platform. SEO and a clear website are the bridge from Web2 to Web3 audience.
- **Where to find them:** Lens Protocol ecosystem, Farcaster ecosystem builders, Mirror.xyz publish community
- **Pitch angle:** "Your community loves the product. Your website doesn't explain it to anyone else."
