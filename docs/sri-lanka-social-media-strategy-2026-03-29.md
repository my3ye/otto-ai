## 7. CREATING THE VIRAL MOMENT: PROPOSAL SUBMISSION DAY

### The Mechanic
The viral moment cannot be manufactured — it has to be genuine. The trigger is the actual proposal submission to the Sri Lankan government. Everything before that is building pressure and audience. Everything after amplifies the response.

### Pre-Submission (Day -7 to -1)
- Tease: "We're submitting a proposal to the Sri Lankan government next week. If it works, the world changes. Thread."
- Post the core thesis thread (Thread A)
- Get 3-5 crypto KOLs to comment/quote before submission
- Build to minimum 500 genuine followers/engagers interested in the story

### Submission Day: The Ritual
1. **Screenshot moment:** The actual submission confirmation — email, portal, or physical photo — shared on X. Real proof.
2. **The opening post:** One sentence. No thread yet.
   > "We just submitted a proposal to the Sri Lankan government to build the world's first Sovereign Intelligence State. [screenshot]"
3. **Wait 2 hours.** Let it get attention. Replies, quote-tweets, shares.
4. **Then drop the full thread** (see Section 7 below).
5. **Simultaneously:**
   - LinkedIn article goes live
   - Farcaster long-form cast goes live
   - Diaspora Telegram groups get seeded
   - Email goes out to the 50-person contact list

### The Goal
One post that crosses three communities simultaneously:
- Crypto Twitter seeing a "country adopting crypto infrastructure" moment
- Sri Lankan diaspora seeing their country taking a bold step
- Global governance nerds seeing the network state thesis play out in real time

If all three communities start sharing to their own networks within 6 hours of submission, the moment is viral.
