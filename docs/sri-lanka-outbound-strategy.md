# Sri Lanka Outbound Strategy — MY3YE / SOS Systems
**Date:** 2026-03-29
**Mission:** Reach Sri Lankan government decision makers, aligned diaspora, IMF contacts, and sympathetic media to propose MY3YE/SOS Systems as the infrastructure layer for Sri Lanka's Digital Economy Strategy 2030.

---

## Strategic Frame (What We're Selling)

We are NOT pitching crypto. We are pitching:
> "A sovereign digital infrastructure stack that creates 50,000 skilled jobs, funds itself through Web3 capital markets, and positions Sri Lanka as the reference implementation for the Global South."

The IMF constraint is a feature, not a bug. We frame SOS Systems as IMF-compatible: no legal tender proposal, no mandatory adoption, infrastructure-as-public-good. This is the exact frame that got Bhutan, UAE, and Singapore their blockchain programs through IMF scrutiny.

**The ask at each stakeholder level differs:**
- Government: **Partnership + policy sandbox**
- Diaspora: **Champion + introducer**
- IMF contacts: **Framing validator + gatekeeper opener**
- Journalists: **Narrative amplifier**

## Warm Intro Paths via Crypto Networks

### Path 1: Port City Colombo (CPCEC) → Ministry
- **How:** Register or express interest with CPCEC's investor relations unit (invest@portcitycolombo.lk). CPCEC has a direct MOU with Ministry of Digital Economy. Any serious investor expression gets routed to ministry level.
- **Why it works:** CPCEC is desperate for lighthouse tenants. A credible Web3/AI firm wanting to anchor in Port City gives them a win. They will make the introduction.
- **Next step:** Submit a one-page investment expression letter to CPCEC investor relations.

### Path 2: SLSVN Annual Sri Lanka Day (Silicon Valley) → Diaspora Champions
- **How:** SL Silicon Valley Network holds annual events; speakers include diaspora founders AND Sri Lankan government officials (Ministry of Finance/Digital Economy regularly sends representation).
- **Why it works:** Both the diaspora champion AND the government contact are in the same room. One warm introduction at this event is worth 50 cold emails.
- **Next step:** Identify next SLSVN event date; request speaker slot or sponsor tier (typically accessible for startups).

### Path 3: Polkadot / Web3 Foundation → IMF Digital Money Team
- **How:** W3F has submitted position papers to IMF Digital Money working groups. ONEON's identity proposal for Polkadot puts MY3YE in the W3F network. W3F has direct relationships with the IMF MCM team.
- **Why it works:** IMF Digital Money team is NOT the same as the Sri Lanka country desk. They're more ideologically open and can help frame proposals in a way that country desks accept.
- **Next step:** Post on Polkadot forum (as already planned for ONEON grant) and directly message W3F policy team about SL infrastructure proposal.

### Path 4: UNDP Sri Lanka → Green Bond Framework → Finance Ministry
- **How:** UNDP has a resident office in Colombo and co-authored Sri Lanka's Climate Finance Strategy 2025-2030. They have a mandate to facilitate private-sector proposals for green bond issuance. Tusita eco-community qualifies under 3+ of the 12 identified instruments.
- **Why it works:** UNDP provides a multilateral legitimacy wrapper that makes the Finance Ministry meeting easier to get. "UNDP-facilitated" = not a cold approach.
- **Next step:** Email UNDP Sri Lanka country office with a Tusita sustainable community brief.

### Path 5: British Sri Lanka Chamber of Commerce (BSLCC) → UK Diaspora → High Commission
- **How:** BSLCC has active programs connecting UK diaspora investors to Sri Lanka BOI. They facilitate introductions for exactly this type of FDI proposal.
- **Why it works:** UK government (FCO) actively encourages SL investment as part of post-crisis recovery support. BSLCC + High Commission intro carries diplomatic weight.
- **Next step:** Apply for BSLCC membership ($150-200 annual); attend a networking event; pitch the proposal within the chamber context.

### Path 6: Crypto Twitter / LinkedIn → Journalists
- **How:** Several key journalists who cover nation-state crypto (Nikhilesh De at CoinDesk, Casey Michel at various outlets, Ramy Inocencio at various outlets) are active on Twitter/X and respond to well-framed DMs with actual news value.
- **Why it works:** Journalists need stories. A credible, documented proposal with real government engagement (even early-stage) is a legitimate story pitch. Being first to the story matters to them.
- **Next step:** When we have the first government response (even a meeting confirmation), that's the hook. Pitch then, not before.

## What NOT to Do

- **Don't lead with "blockchain" or "crypto"** to government contacts. Lead with jobs, investment, and Digital Economy Strategy alignment.
- **Don't send the full whitepaper cold.** A 2-page executive summary that generates a reply is worth more than 40 pages that gets ignored.
- **Don't approach CBSL asking for crypto regulation.** Approach CBSL asking to be compliant with whatever framework they're building.
- **Don't pitch journalists before there's a government conversation.** "We want to propose something" is not a story. "We had a meeting with [ministry]" is.
- **Don't contact the President's office cold.** It will be routed to a form response. Use the warm intro paths first.
