## Part I: The Problem

### What Extractive Tourism Does

The global resort industry is one of the most efficient extraction mechanisms ever designed.

A family sells ancestral land for a fraction of its value. A resort chain builds. Guests arrive from Europe, North America, Southeast Asia — paying $300 a night. Workers from the adjacent village earn $12 a day. The difference — the compounding margin between what visitors pay and what the community receives — flows to a corporate ledger in a jurisdiction the community has never visited.

This is not exceptional. It is the standard model. It has operated this way for decades. And it accelerates when tourism grows: higher demand, higher prices, the same labor rates, the same extraction curve.

Real estate compounds the problem. Land near tourism infrastructure appreciates. That appreciation accrues to the landowner — not the community member who built the resort, served the guest, or maintained the grounds for twenty years. The person who contributed the most to the asset's value captures the least of the asset's appreciation.

Three patterns define extractive tourism:

1. **Revenue extraction** — income generated on community land flows to external capital.
2. **Governance exclusion** — the people who live and work there have no say in how it operates.
3. **Asset concentration** — appreciation and ownership accrue to capital, not labor.

Tusita is designed to invert all three.

---

### What Extractive Real Estate Does

Real estate is not inherently extractive. What makes it extractive is the ownership model.

When ownership is concentrated — in a developer, a hedge fund, a REIT, a family dynasty — appreciation is also concentrated. The renter who builds their life in a place has no claim on the place appreciating around them. The construction worker who built the building has no claim on the building's value over time. The community that made the location desirable has no claim on the desirability they created.

Tokenized real estate has mostly failed to solve this. Most RWA platforms have simply fractionalized the same concentrated ownership structure — giving more people access to the same extractive model. Smaller slices of the same pie.

Tusita is designed around a different premise: the people who build, maintain, and govern the land are the people who own it. Not metaphorically. On-chain. With verifiable, non-dilutable, protocol-enforced rights.

## Part III: The Architecture

### Community Ownership Model — How It Works

Tusita land governance operates through a dual-layer architecture. Neither layer is metaphorical. Both are enforced by protocol.

**Layer 1: Land NFTs (Fractional Community Ownership)**

Each Tusita location is divided into Land NFTs representing fractional ownership stakes. These are Real World Assets (RWA) — on-chain representations of actual legal ownership, with smart contracts enforcing governance rights and revenue distribution.

Land NFTs are available through three paths:

| Acquisition Path | Who | What They Get |
|-----------------|-----|---------------|
| Community Purchase | Any participant | Land NFT: fractional ownership stake, proportional revenue share, governance weight |
| Labor Contribution | Physical builders, farmers, operators | CET (Contribution Equity Tokens) earned during construction/operations, convertible to Land NFTs after Phase 2 |
| Founding Contributor | Core community members | Discounted Land NFTs at Phase 0 pricing before external capital |

**Layer 2: $TUSITA Token (Governance + Treasury)**

$TUSITA is the governance token of the Tusita DAO. It controls the community treasury, approves new sites, sets contribution weightings, and governs operational policy.

$TUSITA is not a financial instrument. It does not represent a share of profit. It represents voice in how the system operates. Revenue sharing is a separate mechanism — encoded in the Land NFT contract, not the governance token.

This separation matters. Governance and economic participation are distinct. An investor can hold Land NFTs without holding $TUSITA governance weight. A contributor can hold governance weight through DPC score without holding Land NFTs. Both are legitimate members of the system. Neither is privileged over the other by design.

**The DPC Score (Decentralized Proof of Contribution)**

Governance weight in the Tusita DAO is not one-token-one-vote. It is earned through contribution via the DPC formula: `P = f(Is, Ec, Rw)` — where Is is Structural Impact, Ec is Consistent Energy (sustained engagement), and Rw is Weighted Resonance (influence on other contributors).

DPC scores accrue through:
- Physical labor (construction, maintenance, agriculture) — verified via Multi-Party Attestation Protocol
- Digital contributions (code, design, content) — verified via on-chain activity
- Community operations (site management, hospitality, education) — verified via peer attestation

The non-linear DPC formula prevents governance capture through any single dimension. A wealthy participant who buys Land NFTs but never contributes cannot accrue the governance weight of a builder who worked on-site for two years.

### The Labor Contribution Economy

This is the mechanism that makes Tusita different from every other "tokenized real estate" or "eco-resort with community benefits" pitch.

**Contribution Equity Tokens (CET)**

Workers who contribute to Tusita's physical construction, agricultural operations, hospitality operations, or community infrastructure earn CET — Contribution Equity Tokens.

CET are soulbound (non-transferable). They represent your specific contribution to a specific asset. They cannot be sold or traded. They can be converted to Land NFTs at a protocol-defined rate after Phase 2 milestones close — giving long-term contributors a direct path to ownership of what they helped build.

**The Income Path for Physical Workers (Critical)**

CET does not only represent future ownership. It generates present income.

For every Tusita location, the smart contract governing that site's revenue distributes:
- **40% to the Community Treasury** (governed by $TUSITA holders for ongoing development)
- **35% to Land NFT holders** (proportional to ownership stake)
- **25% to CET holders** (proportional to contribution score for that specific site)

CET distributions are paid in stablecoins (USDC/USDT) on a quarterly schedule. A construction worker who helped build Dome 1 at the first Tusita site earns quarterly stablecoin income from that dome's revenue — not someday, not in abstract governance tokens — in real money, every quarter, proportional to what they built.

This is not charity. It is not marketing copy. It is encoded in the contract. The worker who poured the foundation earns from the foundation. Permanently.

The CET redemption mechanism: if a contributor needs liquidity before Phase 2 land NFT conversion, the DAO maintains a buyback pool funded by 5% of treasury inflows. Contributors can redeem CET at fair value (established by a 90-day revenue multiple). This option exists. It is not guaranteed at any specific price — but the mechanism closes the gap between "symbolic equity" and real income.

### RWA Token Structure

The Tusita RWA stack has three layers. Each layer is distinct. None is optional.

**Layer 1 — Land NFTs (ERC-721 / equivalent)**
- One NFT = one fractional ownership unit of a specific Tusita location
- Carries: proportional revenue rights (35% of site net), governance weight (Land NFT DPC entry), and legal claim on underlying land (enforced through off-chain legal structure + on-chain hash of title deed)
- Price at Phase 0 community round: $500–$5,000 per unit (tiered by location, size, and contribution record)
- Total supply per site: capped at 1,000 units (prevents dilution, ensures governance coherence)

**Layer 2 — $TUSITA (ERC-20 / equivalent)**
- DAO governance token
- Earned through: DPC contribution (merit-based), community purchase at fair launch, grant allocations
- No private investor allocation (no pre-sale, no VC cliff)
- Governs: new site approvals, contribution weight updates, treasury deployment, protocol upgrades

**Layer 3 — CET (Soulbound / ERC-5192)**
- Labor equity tokens — non-transferable, site-specific
- Earned through verified contributions during Phase 1 and Phase 2 construction
- Income rights: 25% of site net revenue, quarterly, stablecoin
- Conversion: CET → Land NFT at protocol rate after Phase 2 site milestones
- Buyback: DAO maintains 5%-of-treasury buyback pool for CET liquidity

**Blockchain Strategy**

Tusita's contracts are planned for deployment on a community-ratified chain — EVM-compatible (Arbitrum preferred for Phase 1 due to low fees, Ethereum L1 for legal record-of-title NFT anchoring). Cross-chain compatibility via ONEON (planned). No chain exclusivity. The community, not the investor, decides where contracts live.

## Part VI: On-Chain Governance of Land

Land governance at Tusita is not managed by a corporate entity. It is managed by a DAO — the Tusita Sovereignty Protocol — with protocol-enforced rules that no individual, investor, or administrator can unilaterally override.

**What the DAO Governs**
- New site approvals (requires 2/3 vote of $TUSITA governance weight)
- Treasury deployment (requires simple majority, with 7-day timelock)
- Contribution weight adjustments (DPC component ratios — requires 3/4 supermajority)
- Operational policy (pricing, management, site rules — requires simple majority)
- Land NFT parameters (supply cap changes, new release pricing — requires 2/3 vote)

**What the Protocol Enforces Without a Vote**
- Revenue distribution (35/25/40 split at contract level — cannot be changed without DAO supermajority)
- CET income rights (quarterly stablecoin distributions — automated by smart contract)
- Governance weight calculations (DPC formula — immutable except by protocol upgrade vote)
- Investor governance cap (5% maximum without DPC contribution — enforced in contract)

**Off-Chain Legal Structure**
On-chain governance requires off-chain legal infrastructure. The Tusita land ownership model uses:
- A land trust structure (offshore, jurisdiction TBD — Isle of Man or Cayman Islands are candidates for stability and legal framework)
- The land trust holds title to each Tusita site on behalf of the DAO
- The trust agreement encodes the DAO's governance rights in legal language
- On-chain governance decisions are mirrored in trust amendments (with a 30-day implementation window)
- The hash of the title deed and trust agreement is anchored to Ethereum L1 as a permanent record

This hybrid structure is not a workaround. It is the correct architecture for real-world land governance with on-chain enforcement. Neither the legal structure nor the smart contract alone is sufficient. Together, they create a system where neither bad actors nor institutional overreach can override community rights.
