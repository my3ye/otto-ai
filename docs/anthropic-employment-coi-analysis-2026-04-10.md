## Part 1: IP Assignment (PIIA) — The Core Legal Risk

### What a PIIA Does

All Anthropic employees sign a Proprietary Information and Inventions Assignment agreement. No public copy of Anthropic's specific PIIA exists, but the structure is standard across Silicon Valley AI companies:

- Assigns to Anthropic **all inventions** that are:
  - (a) created using company resources, equipment, or facilities
  - (b) created during employment hours
  - (c) **relating to the company's business or anticipated R&D** ← the critical clause
  - (d) resulting from employee's work duties

- Includes a **Prior Inventions Schedule** — the only legal mechanism to carve existing work out from employer claims
- Requires disclosure of outside business activities and written approval to continue them
- Confidentiality obligations survive termination indefinitely

### California Labor Code Section 2870 — The Employee Shield

California law protects employees' right to work on side projects developed **entirely on own time, without using employer resources** — BUT with one crucial exception:

> The protection does **NOT** apply where the invention "relates at the time of conception or reduction to practice to the employer's business, or actual or demonstrably anticipated research or development of the employer."

**Analysis for Ottolabs:**

Ottolabs builds AI-powered services (WebAssist, Otto) using Claude as the foundation. Anthropic's business is building and operating Claude, and enabling developers to build on it commercially. As of April 8, 2026, Anthropic also **directly operates** a managed AI agent hosting service (Claude Managed Agents). A California court would have a very strong basis to find Ottolabs "relates to" Anthropic's business regardless of when or where the work was done.

**Jurisdiction note:** Section 2870 only applies under California law. If Mev is employed remotely from Sri Lanka and the contract specifies Sri Lankan law, this protection does not exist at all. California law must be specified as governing law to get this protection.

### The Prior Inventions Schedule — Mev's Main Defensive Tool

The PIIA's Prior Inventions Schedule is a section where employees list work they created *before* employment that they do not assign to the employer. **If Ottolabs is not listed here before signing, Anthropic may have grounds to claim ownership of everything Ottolabs develops during the employment period.**

What must appear on the schedule:
- Full name: "Ottolabs" (and trade names: WebAssist, Otto)  
- Clear description: "AI agent platform providing autonomous web, operational, and branding services to SMBs; includes Otto multi-agent system and WebAssist AI website builder"
- Documented creation date: **earliest git commit date, domain registration date, any prior revenue records**
- Statement that it is excluded from assignment

The schedule is negotiable at the offer stage. It is not negotiable after day one.

## Part 2: Moonlighting Policy — The Operational Risk

### Industry Baseline (No Anthropic-Specific Public Policy Exists)

Standard language in tech offer letters:
> "While you render services to the Company, you will not engage in any other gainful employment, business, or activity without the written consent of the Company."

Failure to disclose is universally treated as dishonesty, not oversight — grounds for termination regardless of whether the side business itself would have been approved.

Anthropic is known to use a formal **conflict-of-interest clearance form** for employees who consult or serve on nonprofit boards, confirming a structured disclosure process exists. Ottolabs would go through the same (or a more rigorous) process.

### The "Company Time and Resources" Tripwire

Even with written approval, the ongoing operational risk is:
- Using Anthropic computers, VPNs, or systems for any Ottolabs work
- Working on Ottolabs during Anthropic working hours
- Sharing any Anthropic-internal knowledge (model capabilities, roadmap, pricing, usage patterns) that benefits Ottolabs
- Using Anthropic's name or relationship for Ottolabs commercial benefit without approval

Any of these converts an approved side business into a fireable misconduct violation.

## Part 3: Anthropic API Terms — Ottolabs Is Currently Clean

Anthropic's Commercial Terms of Service prohibit:
- Building a competing product or service, including training competing AI models
- Reselling API access except as expressly approved

**Ottolabs is NOT in violation.** WebAssist and Otto are AI-powered services built on Claude — the exact use case Anthropic's API enables. The prohibition targets companies building rival LLMs or reselling raw API access.

The tension is in the **employment agreement**, not the API terms. These are two separate legal relationships.

**However:** Now that Anthropic offers Claude Managed Agents (April 8, 2026), the strategic overlap has increased. Anthropic is now a direct competitor to businesses like Ottolabs in the AI agent services layer. This doesn't change API terms compliance, but it makes the PIIA "relates to business" analysis harder to argue against, and could create pressure internally for Mev to divest or wind down Ottolabs post-hire.

## Part 6: Questions Mev Must Answer Before Applying

### Legal/Structural (P0 — Must Answer Before Offer)

1. **Is Ottolabs incorporated?** If not: incorporate *before* applying. An incorporated legal entity with registration dates is far harder to claim ownership of than a personal project. This is step zero.

2. **What is the earliest documented proof of Ottolabs' existence?** The Prior Inventions Schedule needs specific dates. Compile: earliest git commit (check `git log --reverse`), domain registration dates (webassist.ink, otto.lk), any prior revenue records, founding documents.

3. **Are you willing to retain an employment IP attorney to review the PIIA before signing?** Cost: $500–$2,000. Non-negotiable given the stakes. This is not optional.

4. **What governing law do you want in the contract?** California law preferred (Section 2870 protection). If hired remotely under Sri Lankan law, you lose this protection entirely.

### Operational (P1 — Must Clarify Before Starting)

5. **How autonomous is Otto today?** If Mev is manually managing daily Ottolabs operations, a full-time Anthropic role creates a time conflict that can't be papered over. Otto must be autonomous enough that Mev's involvement is strategic (weekly), not operational (daily).

6. **Will you separate all Ottolabs infrastructure from any Anthropic-provided devices or accounts?** Ottolabs must run exclusively on separate hardware, its own API keys, its own email, its own GitHub account. Zero crossover.

7. **What is the minimum Ottolabs revenue needed to feel comfortable not divesting?** If Anthropic pressure to wind down materializes, at what revenue level would Ottolabs be worth fighting for vs. accepting a buyout or transition?

### Strategic (P2 — Inform the Offer Negotiation)

8. **Which Anthropic role specifically?** A role in the developer ecosystem/API platform team creates much greater conflict than a role in fundamental research or trust and safety. The degree of conflict is role-specific.

9. **What's your exit timeline if you take the job?** If the plan is 2-3 years at Anthropic to build credibility then return full-time to Ottolabs, that changes the calculus vs. treating Anthropic as a permanent role.

10. **How do you feel about Anthropic potentially claiming credit for or restricting Ottolabs during your tenure?** This is a real possibility. If Ottolabs is a non-negotiable, Anthropic employment may require a hard cap on Ottolabs' growth trajectory during the employment period.

## Part 7: The Four Things to Negotiate in Writing

If Mev applies and receives an offer, these four items must be in writing before signing. Verbal "that's fine" from a recruiter is legally meaningless.

1. **Prior Inventions Schedule** — Ottolabs (WebAssist, Otto) listed by full name, description, and earliest documentation date. Explicitly carved out from PIIA assignment.

2. **Written moonlighting approval** — a letter or addendum from Anthropic confirming Ottolabs can continue operating under defined conditions (no company time, no company resources, no confidential information).

3. **API account confirmation** — that Ottolabs' commercial Claude API account (separate from any Anthropic employee account) may continue to be used commercially for Ottolabs products.

4. **Governing law** — California law specified, or at minimum an acknowledgment that Section 2870 protections apply regardless of work location.
