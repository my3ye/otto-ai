## Part I: The Entity

### What Ottolabs Is Designed to Build

Ottolabs is designed to be the physical arm of the MY3YE ecosystem — the layer that transforms digital intelligence into sovereign physical infrastructure. Four verticals are planned:

**Device Ecosystem (Planned)**
A suite of purpose-built hardware designed for community sovereignty: Otto Puck (portable compute node), Otto Band (biometric wearable + health monitoring), Otto Phone (ONEON-native communication device), Otto Glasses (AR interface — field display, spatial compute, ONEON-linked), Otto Ring (payments + identity + health: tap to pay, biometric presence, SpO2/HR). Every device is planned to run a purpose-scaled AI chip. The goal: hardware that serves the person carrying it, not the platform selling it.

**Robotics (Four Tiers Planned)**
Humanoid (general-purpose labor), Industrial (manufacturing automation), Agricultural (vertical farms, aquaponics, permaculture operations), Micro-swarm (precision agriculture and environmental monitoring). The robotics layer is designed around RaaS — Robotics-as-a-Service — a model that makes recurring revenue predictable for investors and affordable for operators.

**Otto Factories (DAO-Governed Manufacturing, Planned)**
Physical manufacturing facilities designed to be governed by contributor DAOs, not corporate boards. The labor that builds them earns equity. The communities that host them govern them. The revenue that flows from them is redistributed by protocol, not by corporate discretion.

**Otto Agriculture + Energy (Planned)**
Vertical farms, aquaponics systems, and permaculture land integrated with Tusita communities. Distributed renewable energy grid — solar and battery nodes — designed to make each Tusita location energy-independent.

### What Tusita Is Designed to Be

Tusita is designed as a network of sovereign island communities. Not a resort chain. Not a co-living startup. A parallel civilization — physically inhabitable, contributor-governed, architecturally sacred. Large domes incorporating the culture of all traditions. Dedicated places of worship. A meditation center at each location. The physical home of the people building this ecosystem.

Tusita is also an income engine. External travelers and guests generate revenue. The community governs the space. Income flows back to contributors and community treasury.

**Current status:** Tusita is in design and planning phase. No land has been acquired. No construction has begun. The website (tusita.xyz) describes the vision.

Three audiences. Three entry points. One architecture.

### For Hardware Investors (Lemnos, Playground Global, Khosla)

The sovereign manufacturing thesis is the dominant 2026 macro theme. Every major economy is asking the same question: who controls the physical layer of intelligence? Ottolabs is designed to answer it from the bottom up — not a national program, but a contributor-governed network of factories, farms, and devices that serve the people who build them.

The RaaS model makes this legible: recurring revenue, predictable unit economics, operator affordability. The sovereign manufacturing angle makes it timely. The on-chain governance makes it verifiable.

What we need from a hardware investor: a $500K–$3M seed check, supply chain expertise, and tolerance for a 36-month infrastructure timeline. What we offer in return: milestone-gated tranches enforced by smart contract (capital cannot be misappropriated before delivery proof), labor-first equity distribution (workers earn CET before investor SAFEs convert), and governance non-concentration protection (no investor controls more than 5% governance weight without DPC contribution).

The Ottolabs story is not a consumer hardware pitch. It is a sovereign manufacturing infrastructure play. Frame it that way.

### For Green Bond / Development Finance (UNDP, ADB, IDB)

Tusita is not an eco-resort. It is a proof of concept for sovereign community development — what happens when physical spaces are governed by their residents, funded by verified environmental impact, and integrated with AI systems that serve the people living there.

The numbers align with the mandate: ecotourism grows at 14.31% CAGR to $945B by 2034 (IMARC Group, 2024). ESG-credentialed properties trade 15–20% above standard assets (JLL / CBRE ESG Research, 2024). The Tusita pitch for development finance is: PPP structure, carbon credit revenue, eco-tourism fees, contributor governance, and AI-integrated resource management. The ask is a framework partnership, not a grant — Tusita generates its own revenue once operational.

### For Web3 / DePIN Investors

The ONEON network is designed to run on sovereign hardware — Otto devices at the edge, contributor-governed nodes at scale. The DePIN model has been pioneered by Helium, IoTeX, and Hivemapper. ONEON adds identity, communications, and governance primitives that the existing DePIN infrastructure does not provide.

The market gap is documented. The architecture is designed.

## Part IV: The Anti-Extraction Architecture

This is not a governance section added for optics. It is the structural reason this capital strategy is fundable on sovereign terms.

**On-Chain Treasury with Milestone Gates**
Every capital raise from Phase 2 onward uses milestone-gated disbursement via multisig treasury contracts (planned for deployment). Investor capital enters the treasury. Each tranche releases only on attested milestone completion. The attestation requires multi-party verification — project team + independent validator + community oracle. No milestone, no release. The investor can verify this on-chain. No trust required.

**CET — Contribution Equity Tokens (Planned)**
Physical workers who contribute to building Ottolabs infrastructure are designed to earn soulbound Contribution Equity Tokens. These tokens encode labor equity — they cannot be sold, diluted by investor SAFEs, or transferred away. The people who built it own it. This is designed to be enforced at the contract layer, not aspirationally promised in a whitepaper.

**30% Agent Tax (Planned)**
When AI agents complete tasks that would otherwise require human labor, 30% of the efficiency gain is designed to flow back into the contributor pool for redistribution. Automation gains do not go to capital. They return to the system. This is encoded in the planned smart contracts, not managed by corporate policy. The measurement oracle design is in progress — initial implementation will use logged task completion times benchmarked against documented manual rate equivalents.

**Governance Non-Concentration Clause**
Built into every investment agreement from Phase 2 onwards: no investor controls more than 5% of project governance without corresponding DPC contribution. SAFEs convert to economic participation, not governance control. Capital buys a share of the value. It does not buy the right to steer.
