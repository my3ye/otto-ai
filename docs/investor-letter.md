## The Thesis

Power is not a lake. Power is a river.

In a lake system, value accumulates at the center and stagnates. The owner of the lake sets the price of entry. The more users depend on the lake, the higher that price rises. The users who fill the lake enrich the owner. The owner's leverage compounds. This is not a conspiracy. This is platform economics, operating as designed.

In a river system, value moves toward contribution. The more you build, the more you earn. The more you give, the stronger the current carries you. The rules are not held — they are encoded. The machine needs no priest. No administrator who can change the terms when it is convenient. No landlord who extracts rent from the desirability you created. The protocol runs whether or not you trust the people who built it.

MY3YE is building the river.

Not as a philosophical stance. As an engineering decision. Every protocol in the ecosystem is designed to make the river the only viable architecture — where contribution is the currency that compounds, and extraction is structurally penalized by the math.

The question for any capital allocator in this moment is the question that existed in 1995 and 2003 and 2010: are you funding the base that the next wave depends on — or the landlord that will rent it back to them?
