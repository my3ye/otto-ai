**Created:** 2026-03-27
**Purpose:** Contract/freelance pipeline for Mev — Full Stack / Frontend engineer with Supra Oracles pedigree
**Channel:** LinkedIn DM, Twitter DM, Cold Email
**Goal:** 1-3 paid contracts within 3-4 weeks

---

## Positioning Foundation

### Who Mev Is (For Outreach Purposes)
- **Engineering Lead, Supra Oracles** — L1/oracle infrastructure. This is the credential that opens doors in Web3. Oracles are foundational infrastructure; building there means Mev has worked at the intersection of consensus, data feeds, and smart contract integration.
- **Frontend Lead, Flagship.fyi** — Product-grade frontend shipping. Not just protocol theory — can take a product from design to production.
- **Currently building:** Otto AI (autonomous agent OS), WebAssist (live AI-built website product), Koink.fun (chain-agnostic meme protocol). Active builder, not job-hunting out of desperation.

### The Core Positioning Angle
> "I built infrastructure at Supra Oracles and shipped products at Flagship. Now I'm building my own protocols. I take contracts when the work is interesting."

This framing is the opposite of desperate. It positions Mev as a builder who chooses projects, not a job-seeker who needs work. It also creates natural curiosity ("what are you building?") that opens a conversation.

### What NOT to Say
- "Looking for opportunities" — signals desperation
- "I'm available for freelance work" — sounds transactional
- "Let me know if you need help" — puts all the weight on them
- Long bio dumps — nobody reads them

## Outreach Sequence Architecture

### Enrollment Trigger (What Activates Outreach)
Only reach out when a company shows at least ONE of these signals:
1. Active job posting for Frontend Lead, Full Stack, or similar within last 30 days
2. Recent funding announcement (Series A+) in last 60 days
3. New product/protocol launch in last 30 days
4. Founder/CTO posted publicly about "need frontend help" or "hiring" on Twitter
5. Mutual connection who can intro Mev directly

**Never spray-and-pray.** Signal-first only.

### 10-Touch Sequence Over 3 Weeks

```
Touch 1 (Day 1) — Twitter: Follow + like 2-3 posts. No message yet.
Touch 2 (Day 2) — Twitter DM: Short, signal-based opener. (see template)
Touch 3 (Day 3) — LinkedIn: Connection request with 1-line note.
Touch 4 (Day 5) — LinkedIn DM: Value-add message. (see template)
Touch 5 (Day 7) — Twitter: Reply to a relevant tweet from them with a genuine insight.
Touch 6 (Day 10) — Email: Full cold email if email is findable. (see template)
Touch 7 (Day 12) — Twitter: Share something relevant to their stack/problem. Tag them optionally.
Touch 8 (Day 14) — LinkedIn: Second touchpoint — share a short insight or ask a genuine question.
Touch 9 (Day 17) — Email (if no reply): New angle — different pain point.
Touch 10 (Day 21) — Final: Short breakup message. Leave door open.
```

**Daily quota:** 5 new accounts enrolled per day. At 10 touches/account, that's ~50 active touch-points in the system at any time.
**Expected output:** 5 accounts × 5 days = 25 sequences → 4-8% positive reply rate → 1-2 conversations → 1 contract.

## Message Templates

---

### Template 1 — Twitter DM (First Touch)
**Use:** After following and engaging with their content for 1 day.
**Character limit:** Keep under 280 characters ideally.

**Version A — Funding/Launch Signal:**
```
Congrats on the [round/launch] — the [specific thing: oracle integration, agent architecture, etc.] piece is interesting. I did similar work at Supra Oracles. If you're building out the frontend/full stack side, worth a quick chat?
```

**Version B — Hiring Signal:**
```
Saw you're looking for a frontend lead. Built the same stack at Supra Oracles + shipped product at Flagship.fyi — now building [Otto/Koink] between contracts. Open to a quick call if the timing works?
```

**Version C — Technical Hot Take (Warm Open):**
```
Your thread on [topic] landed. That's exactly the tradeoff I ran into at Supra on the [relevant feature]. Solved it [one sentence how]. Happy to share more if useful.
```

**Rules:**
- No "I" as the first word
- No pitch in the first message — curiosity or insight only
- One ask maximum — "worth a chat?" not "can we schedule a 30-min call?"

---

### Template 2 — LinkedIn DM
**Use:** After connection request is accepted. (Send connection request with note, then follow up within 24h.)

**Connection Request Note (< 300 chars):**
```
Saw [company] is building in [space]. I led engineering at Supra Oracles and shipped product at Flagship — now running my own protocols between contracts. Wanted to connect.
```

**Follow-Up DM (after connection accepted):**

**Version A — Specific Problem Angle:**
```
Thanks for connecting.

Noticed [company] is [specific thing: scaling the frontend team / launching the new DEX interface / building out the agent SDK]. I ran into the exact same challenge at Supra — [one sentence on the specific challenge and how it was approached].

Not sure if the timing is right for you, but I take the occasional contract when the work is interesting. Worth a 15-minute call to see if there's a fit?

— Mev
webassist.ink (my current build)
```

**Version B — Clean Ask:**
```
Thanks for connecting.

Quick context: Engineering Lead at Supra Oracles → Frontend Lead at Flagship.fyi → now building Otto AI and Koink.fun. I take contracts on the side when the project matches.

If you're working on [relevant specific area] and need senior frontend/full stack capacity, I'd be up for a conversation.

Anything in the works?

— Mev
```

**Rules:**
- Always end with a question — forces a response decision
- Keep to 5-7 lines maximum
- Hyperlink one credibility anchor (webassist.ink or LinkedIn profile) but not both
- Never attach a resume — it signals job-seeker, not contractor

### Template 3 — Cold Email
**Use:** After Twitter + LinkedIn have been attempted OR when email is the only available channel.

**Subject line options (pick based on signal):**
- `re: the frontend team` (if they posted about hiring)
- `Supra Oracles → [their company name]` (direct credibility bridge)
- `quick question on [specific feature they shipped]`
- `Web3 frontend — worth 15 min?`

**Body:**

```
Hi [First Name],

[Saw you just raised a Series B / Saw the [product name] launch / Noticed you're building out the [agent SDK / oracle integration / DeFi UI]] — the [specific technical detail] stood out to me.

I built [relevant parallel work] at Supra Oracles (oracle data feeds, frontend infrastructure, cross-chain integrations). Then led the frontend side at Flagship.fyi — took the product from early beta to production. Currently building Otto AI (autonomous agent OS) and Koink.fun between contracts.

If [company] is ramping up on [relevant frontend/full stack area], I take a contract or two when the work is the right fit.

Open to a 15-minute call this week to see if there's anything worth exploring?

— Mev
my3ye.xyz | webassist.ink
```

**Version B — Shorter / More Direct:**
```
Hi [First Name],

Engineering Lead at Supra Oracles, Frontend Lead at Flagship.fyi — now building protocols on the side and contracting in between.

Saw [company] is working on [specific thing]. I've done close to the same work at Supra — [one sentence on specific relevant experience].

Worth a quick call?

— Mev
```

**Rules:**
- Subject line: 3-5 words, lowercase, internal-email feel
- Body: 5-7 lines maximum for Version B, 8-10 for Version A
- One CTA only — a single "15-minute call?" ask
- Always personalize the opening line with a specific observation about their work
- Do NOT include a full resume or LinkedIn profile dump — it triggers recruiter-brain, not hiring-manager-brain

## Supra Oracles Credibility Angles

This credential is the highest-signal item in Mev's background for Web3 outreach. Use it precisely.

### How to Deploy It

**With Oracle/Infrastructure companies:**
> "I built [specific feature: data feed validation / cross-chain oracle infrastructure / frontend for the price feed dashboard] at Supra Oracles."
Use specific technical details — not "I worked at Supra Oracles."

**With AI x Web3 companies:**
> "At Supra, I dealt with the same latency / data freshness / cross-chain coordination problem you're solving. Here's what we did..."
Lead with the problem match, use Supra as the proof point.

**With DeFi protocols:**
> "Oracle integration in DeFi frontends is something I know well from the Supra side — I understand both the contract interface and the UI layer."
Cross-layer credibility — rare and valuable.

**With general Web3 companies:**
> "Engineering Lead at Supra Oracles" is the hook. The job title + company is enough to stop the scroll. Follow it immediately with what you built, not what the company does.

### What to Avoid
- "I worked at Supra Oracles" (passive — sounds like a resume bullet)
- Explaining what Supra Oracles is (if they know, it's redundant; if they don't, you've lost them)
- Listing Supra as one of many credentials (it should be the anchor, not a list item)

## Positioning Script (For Calls / Voice)

If a call happens, use this structure — not a pitch, a conversation:

1. **Open with curiosity:** "Before I get into my background — what's the thing you're most focused on building right now?"

2. **Mirror their answer to your experience:** "That's interesting — at Supra, we had the same challenge with [X]. The approach that worked was [one sentence]."

3. **State your current position:** "Right now I'm building two protocols of my own — Otto AI and Koink.fun — and I take a contract every few months when the work matches my stack."

4. **The ask:** "What does the frontend/full stack side look like at [company] right now? I'm curious if there's overlap."

**Goal of the call:** Don't close — discover. The contract should feel like a natural next step, not a pitch.

## Notes

- **Supra Oracles is the anchor.** Every template puts it in the first 2 lines.
- **Async-friendly by design.** No "can we hop on a call this week?" in first messages — it creates pressure. "Worth a chat?" is lower friction.
- **Web3 hiring culture:** Decision-makers are founder/CTO-level, not HR. They respond to technical credibility and builder identity, not recruiter language. Never use words like "opportunity," "position," "resume," or "cover letter."
- **Current build as credibility:** Otto AI and Koink.fun signal that Mev is actively building, not sitting idle. Mention them — they create conversation hooks and signal ambition without bragging.
- **No follow-up spam.** Max 2 follow-ups per channel after first message. More than that damages the relationship.
