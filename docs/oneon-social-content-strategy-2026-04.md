# ONEON Social Content Strategy — Invisible Web3 Onboarding
*Step 0 output — April 2026 series*

---

## Strategic Brief

**Account:** @ONEON (oneon.ink)
**Objective:** Build ONEON audience among Web3-native builders + demonstrate the invisible Web3 onboarding proposition to non-technical audiences simultaneously
**Timeframe:** April 3–16, 2026 (14 posts, 1/day)
**Trigger:** Follows MY3YE ONEON Reveal post (#6 — April 2)
**Target audience:** Primary — Web3-native builders (ENS/XMTP/Lens/Farcaster users, EVM devs, Solana builders). Secondary — non-technical people who are curious about Web3 but blocked by complexity.

---

## Core Insight

Every Web3 social/identity protocol requires you to already be in Web3 before you can use it.

ENS → need ETH wallet first.
XMTP → need wallet address to message.
Lens → need gas to create a profile.
Farcaster → need ETH to rent a username.

ONEON breaks this. You arrive with a handle and email. Sovereignty assembles itself underneath. The user never touches a wallet unless they choose to.

This is the content series' one job: **make the invisible layer legible without making it technical.**

---

## Audience Intelligence

### Primary — Web3-native builders
- Pain: Fragmentation. Every protocol has its own identity schema. ENS ≠ Lens ≠ Farcaster profile. No portability.
- What catches their eye: Architecture posts, sharp takes on missing infrastructure, ERC-4337 + session keys
- Hook: "The layer no one built yet"

### Secondary — Non-technical curious
- Pain: Friction. Wallet, seed phrase, gas, chain name — all before they can do anything.
- What catches their eye: "You don't need any of that."
- Hook: "We made it disappear"

---

## Narrative Arc (14 Days, 4 Chapters)

```
Chapter 1 — THE PROBLEM (Days 1–3)
"Web3 is broken for real people. Here is why."
→ Document the friction. Make the audience feel the problem.

Chapter 2 — THE GAP (Days 4–6)
"Everyone built pieces. No one built the layer."
→ Show Ethereum's fragments. Name what's missing.
→ ONEON is the answer, not announced yet — just implied.

Chapter 3 — THE ARCHITECTURE (Days 7–10)
"This is how the invisible layer works."
→ Reveal ONEON. Explain the three tiers without jargon.
→ Technical credibility for builders. Clarity for non-technical.

Chapter 4 — THE INVITATION (Days 11–14)
"Arrive as you are. Sovereignty follows."
→ CTA. Waitlist. The vision. No hype.
```

## Brand Compliance Notes

- No emojis, no crypto slang (WAGMI, LFG, DYOR), no exclamation marks
- River/lake metaphor maintained throughout
- No promises of returns or financial outcomes
- CTAs are invitations ("Move with it", "arrive as you are") not commands
- Every post is a standalone piece AND part of the arc — the series rewards followers who read all 14 but does not punish those who see only one
