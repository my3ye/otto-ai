## Strategic Frame

The messaging bible defined the campaign premise. This strategy executes it on Twitter/X.

The empire framing works because it is **architectural, not moral**. We don't accuse anyone. We describe a pattern — the same pattern that appeared in the railroad era, the internet era, the cloud era — and ask one question the incumbents never want asked aloud:

**"Will you support the open system or the empire?"**

The strategy runs in three phases:

1. **Seed the frame** (Week 1, Mar 29–Apr 4) — Single posts that name the pattern without stating the question. Make the audience feel the empire before naming it.
2. **Drop the thread** (Week 2, Apr 5–11) — The flagship 7-tweet thread that builds the full argument and closes with the question.
3. **Activate the sides** (Week 3, Apr 12–19) — Follow-up posts, community engagement, amplify the question. Let the sides form.

## Tone Guidelines for This Campaign

- **Do not name OpenAI, Google, Meta by name.** The framing is architectural. Naming players makes it a beef; keeping it structural makes it a position.
- **Do not use "decentralized" or "Web3."** These words signal tribally to crypto but alienate the broader tech audience. Use: "open," "commons," "contributor-governed," "no priest needed."
- **Do not moralize.** The empire is not evil. It is predictable. This is physics, not punishment.
- **Keep the question exact.** "Will you support the open system or the empire?" — no variations. The phrasing is load-bearing.
- **The question closes, it does not open.** Tweet 6 in the thread arrives after 5 tweets of context. The question cannot be the hook — it must be earned.
