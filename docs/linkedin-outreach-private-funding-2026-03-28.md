## Framing Philosophy

**Conviction first. Credibility second.**

The mistake most founders make on LinkedIn is leading with proof: metrics, traction, technical specs. Sophisticated Web3 investors see 50 of those per week. What they're actually looking for is a founder who believes so clearly in what they're building that the ask feels like an invitation, not a pitch.

This document is built around that posture. The thesis comes first. The evidence follows. The CTA is low friction and mutual.

LinkedIn tone is warmer than X DMs — longer by design, narrative-forward, with shared mission framing. These are not broadcast messages. They're direct conversations that happen to start in writing.

## FOLLOW-UP VARIANT 1 — The Proof Point Follow-Up
**Send:** 7 days after InMail, no reply
**Use:** Variants 1 and 2 (AI×Crypto, Ownership Economy targets)
**Tone:** Calm update, not a nudge. One new data point. Zero urgency theater.

---

**Subject:** `[thread subject] — one update`

---

Hi [Name],

Following up on my note from [date]. No pressure if the timing is off or it's not the right fit.

One thing shipped since then worth mentioning: [specific shipped item — e.g., "ONEON identity layer Phase 1A is deployed" / "WebAssist payment infrastructure live" / "Koink.fun tokenomics standard doc published"].

If the original message was interesting but timing wasn't right, I'm easy to reach. If it's not the right fit, no problem at all — I'd rather you say that than leave it open.

— Mev

---

**Word count:** ~80 words
**Usage notes:**
- Fill `[specific shipped item]` with the most technically credible recent milestone — not a soft update ("we wrote a doc"), a hard one ("deployed," "live," "X tasks completed")
- One follow-up only. A third message without a reply is spam.
- If they engaged with a LinkedIn post or commented on anything in the window: reference that instead of leading with the date. "Saw your comment on [X]" > "Following up from March 28."
- Do not extend the original pitch. The follow-up's job is to re-open the door, not walk through it again.

## FOLLOW-UP VARIANT 2 — The Specific Ask Re-Route
**Send:** 7 days after InMail, no reply — use this variant when you suspect the issue is format, not fit
**Use:** Variant 3 (Mission-aligned angels) — they often engage with ideas but don't act on InMail CTAs
**Tone:** Reframe the ask. Offer a different on-ramp.

---

**Subject:** `[thread subject] — different question`

---

Hi [Name],

Circling back from [date] — no response needed if the timing or fit is off.

Different question: is there a founder in your portfolio or network who's working on anything adjacent — sovereign communities, AI infra, ownership economy primitives? Not looking for intro pressure. Just genuinely trying to find the right early collaborators, and you're one of the best-connected thinkers in the space on this.

Happy to return the context if you're ever tracking something that fits MY3YE's architecture.

— Mev

---

**Word count:** ~90 words
**Why this works:**
- Removes the pressure of a direct investment ask entirely — reframes to "mutual value" and "network favor"
- Serial angels and mission-driven investors are more likely to respond to "help me find the right people" than a second cold pitch
- Creates a reciprocity frame ("return the context") — puts the investor in an advisory role they often prefer before committing capital
- Even a response of "can't think of anyone, but tell me more about X" re-opens the conversation on better terms
- The connection often happens here: they respond, then convert to investor through natural conversation
