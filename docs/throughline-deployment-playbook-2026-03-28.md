## Amplification Tactics

### How the community repeats the line

**The line they will repeat:** "Will you support the open system or the empire?"

Do not ask them to repeat it. Create conditions where they repeat it naturally.

**Tactic 1 — The Fork Post**
On Apr 9 (thread day), Discord gets: "There are two systems being built. You already know which one you'd rather exist in."
No further instruction. Let them respond. Pin the best response.

**Tactic 2 — The Companion Line Drop**
Drop "Build here or rent there." in Discord on Apr 3 — no explanation, no link. When someone asks what it means, respond: "Watch @MY3YE. We're about to explain it." Anticipation builds audience arrival on Apr 9.

**Tactic 3 — The Question Handoff**
After the flagship thread, DM or mention 3–5 aligned builders directly:
"This question is going to matter to your audience too. What's your read?"
Give them something to react to — not a request to share.

**Tactic 4 — The Quote Bait Tweet**
T5 of the flagship thread contains a naturally quotable line:
_"They are not coordinating. They don't have to. The incentives coordinate for them."_
This is the likely quote-tweet target. When it gets quoted, engage meaningfully (not a like — a brief, substantive reply).

**Tactic 5 — The Pitch Close in the Wild**
Every Ottolabs investor conversation during this window ends with the verbal close:
"Will you support the open system — or the empire?"
If they ask "what's the open system?" — that's the pull. Link the thread.

## What Goes First / What Amplifies What

**Sequencing logic:**
1. **my3ye.xyz hero copy** goes first — it is permanent and passive. If someone finds us before Apr 9, the frame is already there.
2. **Twitter/X seed posts** build atmospheric conditions. They make the question feel earned when it lands.
3. **Paragraph article** launches Apr 7 — two days before the flagship thread. It gives the thread long-form credibility and cross-traffic.
4. **Flagship thread** is the campaign center. Everything before amplifies it. Everything after extends it.
5. **Discord** activates community before and during the thread drop. After the thread, it becomes the response layer.
6. **Partner amplification** only happens after the thread is live — credibility first, not reach first. Partners amplify proof, not promises.
7. **Pitch materials** run in parallel from Day 1 — they are evergreen and improve with every post that lands.

## Tone Rules (All Channels)

- **No villain naming.** The empire is a pattern, not a person. OpenAI, Google, Meta are not named.
- **No "decentralized" or "Web3."** Say: open, commons, contributor-governed, sovereign, no priest needed.
- **No moralizing.** The empire is predictable, not evil. Physics, not punishment.
- **The question is exact.** "Will you support the open system or the empire?" — word-for-word, always.
- **The question closes, it does not open.** It earns its place at the end of an argument. Never use it as a hook or opener.
- **Discord is warmer.** More direct, more inviting — but same frame, same question. Community owns it alongside us.
