## The Frame

You are applying as 4 different people — because 4 different buyers need 4 different stories. One CV does not cover all four. Do not submit a generic profile to any of these roles.

| Track | You Are | Buyer Sees | Rate/Comp Target |
|---|---|---|---|
| **FE Engineer** | Infrastructure engineer who builds products | Senior FE with rare oracle+AI pedigree | $150K–$220K remote |
| **Engineering Lead** | A systems thinker who ships and coaches | Lead who needs zero ramp-up | $180K–$280K remote |
| **Product Manager** | The PM who doesn't need engineers to explain feasibility | Technical PM with agentic AI depth | $240K–$350K+ TC |
| **AI Consulting** | Builds what most consultants only describe | Rare implementation-first specialist | $250/hr or $10K/month floor |

**The common thread across all four:** "I have shipped agentic AI systems in production. Most people haven't."

## PART 2 — Application Cadence

**Rule:** Quality-first, not volume-first. One tailored application beats five generic ones. Every submission should include a customized opening that references something specific about the company.

### Weekly Target (Steady State)

| Track | Applications/Week | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **FE Engineer** | 4–6 | A-tier: 2 applications. B-tier: 2-3. Volume of openings (Coinbase especially) supports this. |
| **Engineering Lead** | 2–3 | Fewer pure EL openings. Quality > volume. Never apply as EL + FE to same company simultaneously. |
| **Product Manager** | 2–3 | PM roles require customized cover letters. Aggressive > 3/week risks quality collapse. |
| **AI Consulting** | **0 applications** / **15 outbound contacts** | Consulting is not applied to — it is prospected. Direct outreach only. See Part 4. |

**Total weekly activity:** 8–12 job applications + 15 consulting outreach contacts

**Phasing:**
- **Week 1–2:** A-tier only. Do not dilute into B-tier while A-tier is not exhausted.
- **Week 3+:** Begin B-tier applications as A-tier responses arrive. Do not abandon A-tier sequences.
- **Consulting:** Starts Week 1 in parallel, no waiting.

### Application Day Structure (suggested)

| Day | Activity |
|---|---|
| Monday | 2 FE/EL applications + 3 consulting outreach contacts |
| Tuesday | 2 PM applications + 3 consulting outreach contacts |
| Wednesday | 2 FE applications + 3 consulting outreach contacts |
| Thursday | Review replies + follow up active sequences |
| Friday | 1 EL/PM application + 3 consulting outreach contacts + weekly review |

**Time estimate:** 1.5–2 hours/day. Cap at this. Beyond this, quality degrades.

## PART 4 — AI Consulting Warm Outreach Approach

### The Core Principle

AI consulting leads are not found — they are built. No one applies for a fractional engagement. You prospect for companies that are feeling the pain, then position the conversation as problem-solving, not selling.

**The 2026 Buyer:** Mid-market company (500–999 employees), 6–18 months into an AI pilot that either failed or stalled. CFO is asking for ROI metrics they can't produce. No internal AI architect. Burned by generic consulting decks.

**The Angle That Opens Doors:** *"I build agentic AI systems that most consultants only describe in slide decks."*

### Signal Categories (What Triggers Outreach)

**Tier 1 Signals (act within 24 hours):**
- LinkedIn post from a CEO/CTO: "our AI implementation isn't going as planned" / "looking for AI expertise"
- Job posting for "AI Product Manager" or "Head of AI" at mid-market company — they can't hire FTE, they'd take fractional
- Company blog post about an AI initiative that sounds technically naive (common failure pattern in mid-market)
- Funding announcement + press release mentions "AI-first strategy" — they have budget, no execution plan

**Tier 2 Signals (act within 72 hours):**
- Conference speaking on AI transformation + company size 200-999 employees
- FinTech / Healthcare company posts job for "AI Engineer" with a vague description — indicates they're lost
- New CTO/Chief Digital Officer announced at a mid-market company
- LinkedIn post by a VP: asking for AI tool recommendations or "we're evaluating AI vendors"

### Outreach Sequence for Consulting Leads

**Total: 6 touches over 14 days**

```
Touch 1 (Day 1) — LinkedIn: Connection request with signal-specific note
Touch 2 (Day 2) — LinkedIn DM: Problem-framing opener (no pitch)
Touch 3 (Day 5) — Email: Short case angle + proof point + CTA
Touch 4 (Day 8) — LinkedIn: Share a piece of relevant content with a 1-line observation
Touch 5 (Day 11) — Email: Reframe to different pain angle or different stakeholder
Touch 6 (Day 14) — Breakup message: brief, honest, door open
```

### Message Templates

**LinkedIn Connection Request Note:**
```
Saw [company] is [specific thing: building out an AI initiative / evaluating agent workflows / 
announcing your AI strategy]. I build agentic AI systems in production — might be useful context 
for where you're headed. Wanted to connect.
```

**LinkedIn DM (Day 2 — first response after connection):**
```
Thanks for connecting.

Quick context: I build agentic AI systems — the kind that actually run in production, 
not pilot environments. Background: engineering lead at Supra Oracles (blockchain 
infrastructure for AI), now running Otto AI (multi-agent OS) on the side.

What I find most often: companies invest in AI and hit a wall at the workflow architecture 
layer — the model works, the process doesn't. That's where most pilots stall.

Is that a friction point for [company] right now, or are you past that stage?

— Mev
```

**Cold Email (Day 5 — after LinkedIn + if email findable):**

Subject options (3-5 words, lowercase):
- `re: the AI initiative`
- `agentic AI — mid-implementation`
- `[company name] AI architecture`

Body:
```
Hi [First Name],

[Opening signal: Saw your post about [X] / Read about [company]'s AI strategy / Noticed 
the job posting for [AI role]] — the [specific thing: architecture question / workflow 
redesign challenge / ROI measurement problem] stood out.

This is the layer where most implementations get stuck: the model works, the workflow doesn't.

I build production agentic AI systems — multi-agent orchestration, memory architecture, 
workflow automation that survives production. Background: Engineering Lead at Supra Oracles 
(AI oracle infrastructure), now running Otto AI (autonomous agent OS) as my main project.

I work with a small number of companies on a fractional basis when there's a match.

Worth 20 minutes to see if [company]'s situation matches what I work on?

my3ye.xyz | webassist.ink
```

**Breakup Email (Day 14):**

### Proof Points to Use in Every Consulting Conversation

Adapt to the buyer's context — never recite the whole list:

1. **Agentic orchestration in production:** Otto AI heartbeat system, multi-agent workflow engine, DAG orchestration. Real system, 2-year build. Not a demo.
2. **Cost management in production LLM systems:** Budget gates, model routing decisions, cost-per-call discipline. CFO-gate problem solved in practice.
3. **Memory system architecture:** Episodic/semantic/procedural layers as product capability decisions. This is what separates real agentic systems from chatbots with a persona.
4. **Supra Oracles:** Infrastructure for high-stakes, adversarial, verifiable environments. Translates as: "I've worked at production scale with failure consequences."
5. **WebAssist:** End-to-end AI-built website product. Proof of shipping, not just designing.

**Rate floor: $250/hr. $10,000/month retainer. Do not negotiate below this.** The profile justifies it. Starting low is harder to unwind than holding the line.

## PART 5 — Weekly Tracking Template

Use this as a simple spreadsheet (Google Sheets or Notion table). One tab per track.

### Tab Structure: [Track Name] — e.g., "FE Applications" / "PM Applications" / "Consulting Pipeline"

**Columns:**
```
| Date | Company | Role | Tier | Status | Last Touch | Next Action | Notes |
```

**Status Options:**
- `RESEARCHING` — gathering signal, not yet applied
- `APPLIED` — submitted, awaiting response
- `SEQUENCE_ACTIVE` — consulting outreach sequence running
- `REPLIED` — they responded (any reply)
- `POSITIVE` — genuine interest expressed
- `CALL_BOOKED` — meeting scheduled
- `CALL_DONE` — call completed, evaluation ongoing
- `CLOSED_WON` — offer / contract
- `CLOSED_LOST` — passed / rejected / no reply after sequence complete
- `ON_HOLD` — good fit but wrong timing — re-engage in 60 days

### Weekly Review Checklist (Fridays, 30 minutes)

```
[ ] Count total applications this week: Target ≥ 8
[ ] Count consulting outreach contacts: Target ≥ 15
[ ] Any replies received? → move to POSITIVE, book call within 24h
[ ] Any sequences at Day 8+ with no reply? → send Touch 5 (reframe)
[ ] Any sequences at Day 14? → send breakup message today
[ ] A-tier companies with no response in 2 weeks → add to B-tier requeue
[ ] Review: what 1 thing in last week's messages got the best response?
[ ] Update weekly metrics log (see below)
```

### Weekly Metrics Log

Track these in a separate row each week:

| Week | Apps Sent | Consulting Outreach | Replies (Total) | Positive Replies | Calls Booked | Offers/Contracts |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Week 1 | | | | | | |
| Week 2 | | | | | | |
| Week 3 | | | | | | |
| Week 4 | | | | | | |

**Benchmarks to target:**
- Applications reply rate: 10–20% (you have a rare profile — below 10% means the message isn't matching the role)
- Consulting outreach positive reply rate: 5–10% (signal-triggered outreach should hit this)
- Calls → offers: 40–60% of positive replies should convert to a call; 20–30% of calls to a concrete next step

## Key Rules (Non-Negotiable)

1. **One tailored variant per track.** Never submit a generic CV. The FE CV and the PM CV tell different stories.
2. **Signal before outreach.** Consulting outreach without a specific trigger gets deleted. Find the signal first.
3. **Rate floor: $250/hr.** Never anchor below. Geographic discount is not the client's problem — it's an advantage for you.
4. **A-tier first, always.** Do not dilute time into C-tier targets while A-tier pipeline is not exhausted.
5. **Reply within 24 hours.** Any positive reply has a 48-hour half-life. Respond immediately.
6. **Track everything.** If it's not in the spreadsheet, it didn't happen.
7. **Breakup message every sequence.** Every sequence that hits Day 14 with no reply gets a 1-sentence breakup. Keeps the door open without nagging.
