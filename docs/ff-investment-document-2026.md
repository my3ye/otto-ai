### Specific risks you should understand

**1. Execution risk.** Building a business is hard. I may not move fast enough, make the wrong product decisions, struggle to find clients, or run out of runway before we become profitable. Even a good idea fails without good execution.

**2. Market risk.** The market for AI-powered business services is growing — but it is also crowded. There are well-funded competitors. Small businesses may be slower to adopt AI services than we expect. The market we are targeting may not be large enough to support a significant business.

**3. Competition risk.** Large companies — Wix, Squarespace, Fiverr, established agencies — may improve their own AI capabilities faster than we can differentiate. A better-funded competitor could undercut our pricing or replicate our approach.

**4. Founder risk.** I am the key person in this business. If I am unable to continue — due to health, personal circumstances, or anything else — the business is at serious risk. There is currently no co-founder or team to carry it forward.

**5. Technology risk.** Our services depend on third-party AI providers (Anthropic, OpenAI, and others). If those providers raise prices significantly, change their terms, or become unavailable, our cost structure and delivery model could be disrupted.

**6. Liquidity risk.** This is not a publicly traded stock. You cannot sell your investment on a market. You cannot withdraw it when you need cash. Your investment is locked until either a liquidity event occurs (acquisition, IPO, or structured buyback) or the company fails. That could take many years — or never happen.

**7. Timeline risk.** Even if things go well, you should expect to wait 5–10 years before seeing any return. Early-stage investments are not fast. They are not liquid. If you need money back in less than five years, this is not the right investment for you.

## Section 6 — Investment Terms

**Instrument: SAFE (Simple Agreement for Future Equity)**

A SAFE is not a loan. It is not equity (yet). It is a simple agreement that says: you give us money now, and when we raise our next priced funding round, your investment converts into actual company equity — at terms that protect you.

In plain English: you are betting early. If the company grows and raises more money from investors later, your early bet converts into ownership at a favorable price. If the company never raises another round, your SAFE remains unconverted until a liquidity event or dissolution.

**Key terms:**

| Term | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Valuation cap** | $3,000,000 — the maximum valuation at which your SAFE converts, regardless of the next round's valuation |
| **Discount rate** | 20% — if you prefer a discount calculation over the cap, you get shares at 20% below whatever the next round investors pay |
| **Conversion trigger** | Next priced equity round, acquisition, or IPO |
| **No interest** | This is not a loan. No interest accrues. |
| **No maturity date** | Unlike a loan, there is no date by which we must repay you |
| **No voting rights** | SAFE holders do not vote on company decisions |

## Section 7 — What Happens If Things Go Wrong

**If the company struggles:**
We will tell you before you hear it elsewhere. We will not go quiet. We will tell you what is wrong, what we are doing about it, and what it means for your investment. You will not be surprised.

**If we decide to shut down:**
We will wind down in an orderly way, pay creditors first (as required by law), and distribute any remaining assets to shareholders and SAFE holders on a pro-rata basis. In most early-stage failure scenarios, there are no remaining assets after creditor obligations. This means SAFE holders typically receive nothing in a failure wind-down.

**If we cannot repay:**
A SAFE is not a debt instrument. We have no legal obligation to "repay" it on a schedule. You are an equity investor, not a lender. In the event of failure, you have no legal claim to recover your investment from personal assets. Your downside is limited to what you invested — not more.

**If the relationship strains:**
If at any point you feel you need your money back, come to me directly. I cannot promise to return it — the money will be deployed — but I will talk to you honestly about the situation and we will figure out what is possible together.
