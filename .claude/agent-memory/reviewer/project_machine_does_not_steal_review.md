# "The Machine Does Not Steal. The Machine Inherits."

## Critical Issues
1. **Tusita "generates revenue" — line 56**: "as the site generates revenue" implies Tusita is operational and generating revenue now. Registry status = early/concept. Zero revenue. Change to "as Tusita is designed to generate revenue" or use a clearly hypothetical framing.
2. **Redistribution Pool has no technical anchor**: Capitalized as a proper noun, implying it exists as a named deployed product. No mention of whether it's on-chain, a smart contract, a DB ledger. Add one sentence: "structured as verifiable on-chain distributions, not as a promise." Skeptic score was 5.5/10 — this is the central claim of the article and it floats untethered.
3. **CTA is underspecified**: "Build at my3ye.xyz" gives no entry point. At minimum: "Build at my3ye.xyz — start by contributing to any active vertical."

## Warnings
- **"Stake" undefined** (line 70): "earns you a stake" — is this tokens, equity, revenue share? Pick a word.
- **AI attribution complexity glossed over** (line 40): "every automated output has a chain of origin" is aspirationally true but technically unsolved in current AI (stochastic gradient descent distributes credits, not attributions). One hedging clause would neutralize journalist concern.
- **"Ladder has no ceiling"** (line 72): Ladders structurally have tops. Consider "extends as the system grows" or "rises without end."

## What Not to Touch
- **Opening paradox** (line 10): "The machine does not come for you. The machine comes from you." — best opening in the liberation content series. Standalone tweet-ready.
- **"Extraction wearing automation's clothes"** (line 20): best single line in the article. Do not touch.
- **Kicker** (line 80): "It inherits — and it pays interest, forever." — financial framing distinct from river metaphor. Excellent.
- **Tense discipline**: ZERO DPC violations throughout. Every undeployed mechanic correctly uses "designed to," "is designed to," "will eventually." This is the 8th article reviewed and the first with clean tense discipline from start to finish — note for coder as positive reference.
- **Chef example** (§Second Rung): most accessible concrete illustration of the Loop mechanic in the entire content library. More graspable than furniture designer.

## Scores by Perspective

- **Skeptic**: 5.5/10 — Redistribution Pool floats with no mechanism. Tusita example presumes revenue that doesn't exist. Chef earning forever requires 4 unsolved dependencies (AI quality, attribution accuracy, revenue sustainability, pool funding) — none acknowledged.
