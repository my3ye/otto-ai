Elite investor letter sharpened 2026-03-28. Original ~1,100 words cut to ~510 words.

**Why:** Founders Fund / early-ETH investor caliber requires letter format (no headers), tight prose, structural argument over market stats, no pitch deck structure.

**What was cut:**
- All section headers (converted letter to deck format)
- Project list (Shakrah, Otto Music, Koink.Fun, Panik App — 18 projects dilutes credibility at early stage)
- Full capital phase architecture (Phase 1/2/3 detail, fund targets, dollar amounts) — belongs in follow-up deck
- Market sizing stats (ecotourism CAGR, Sri Lanka visitor count) — weakens structural inevitability argument
- "The sovereign manufacturing thesis is the dominant macro theme of 2026" — asserted, not demonstrated
- Bullet-point anti-extraction section — converted to flowing prose
- "This letter explains what we are building..." — announces instead of doing
- "This is not a consumer hardware pitch..." — defensive framing
- "Open Copyright 2026. Share it." — wrong register for targeted elite letter

**What was kept (strong):**
- Opening pattern (railroad/internet/cloud) — structurally sound
- "Power is not a lake. Power is a river." + lake/river explanation — strongest brand metaphor
- "The machine needs no priest." — earns it
- Honest accounting paragraph — trust-building, rare in pitches
- "Paradise has always been someone else's extraction point" — Tusita's core argument
- "The contract has no CEO to call." — cut from v1 but the anti-extraction prose captures this
- "I built this with one AI agent, on one machine, with no funding and no team." — specific, verifiable, impressive
- "Once empire architecture captures it, displacing it is not a product decision. It is a political one." — strongest line in the letter

**Pattern for future investor letters:**
- No section headers in investor letters — even well-formatted headers read as deck
- 400-600 words only for first-contact letters to elite targets
- Market stats weaken structural arguments — use architecture, not CAGR
- The "honest accounting" pattern (what's live vs. planned) is a trust-differentiator — always keep
- "The question is what side of the architecture you want to be on" > "we're raising $X at $Y"
