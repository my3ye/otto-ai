## Displacement (UNHCR 2025)
- 117.3 million forcibly displaced globally as of mid-2025
- 42.5 million refugees, 67.8 million IDPs, 8.42 million asylum seekers
- End 2025: over 121 million total displaced
- Sudan: 10 million internally displaced — largest IDP crisis on earth
- Gaza: 1.9 million displaced (90% of population), many displaced 10+ times

Sources:
- https://www.unhcr.org/sites/default/files/2025-11/mid-year-trends-report-2025.pdf
- https://www.nrc.no/global-figures
- https://concernusa.org/news/largest-refugee-crises/

## Armed Conflicts (ACLED/ICG 2025-2026)
- 240,000 fatalities from violent conflict globally in 2025 (23% increase YoY)
- Ukraine: 78,000 fatalities — world's deadliest conflict
- Sudan: 20,373 killed Aug 2024–Aug 2025
- Gaza: 69,000+ dead, 170,000+ injured since Oct 2023
- Myanmar: 1,200+ distinct armed groups, most fragmented conflict globally; all 330 townships experienced communications blackout since coup
- 2026 watchlist: Sudan, Palestine, Ukraine, Myanmar remain critical (IRC, ICG, CFR)

Sources:
- https://acleddata.com/conflict-index-2026-watchlist
- https://www.crisisgroup.org/cmt/global/10-conflicts-watch-2026
- https://www.newsweek.com/world-map-shows-deadliest-wars-2025-11179539

## Energy Crisis (March 2026)
- US-Israel strikes on Iran began Feb 28, 2026
- Strait of Hormuz effectively closed — 20% of world's oil supply at risk
- Brent crude hit ~$120, settled at $103+ per barrel (highest since 2022)
- Iran IRGC threatens $200/barrel
- IEA released 400 million barrels emergency reserve — insufficient to replace corridor
- LNG spot prices in Asia nearly doubled; European gas prices up ~50%
- Europe facing "major energy supply crisis" if Hormuz remains closed
- Pakistan (imports 40% of energy) and other LMICs disproportionately hit
- IEA called it the "largest disruption to crude supplies in the history of the global oil market"

## Infrastructure Failures in Conflict Zones
- 2024: Record highest rate of internet shutdowns globally, triggered primarily by conflict
- At least 244 deliberate internet shutdowns in 2025
- Myanmar: 85 shutdowns in 2024; 138 townships cut off by April 2025; all 330 townships affected since coup
- Gaza: cell networks near-totally collapsed; fiber cables severed, towers destroyed, fuel blockades
- Sudan: RSF seized ISPs in Khartoum Feb 2024 → two-thirds of population unreachable for 1+ month
- Sudan: 70-80% of hospitals in conflict areas non-functional
- Gaza: 60% of hospitals non-functional, 1,580+ health workers killed, 720+ attacks on healthcare documented by WHO
- Myanmar earthquake 2025: internet blackout blocked aid coordination in real-time
- Iran: Lancet 2026 documented how internet shutdowns directly harm right to health

## Emergency Services Gaps (Global)
- 4.6 billion people lack access to essential health services (WHO/World Bank 2025)
- 5 billion people lack access to safe, affordable surgical/anesthesia care (Lancet Commission)
- Over 50% of deaths in LMICs could be averted with access to high-quality emergency care
- Low-income countries spend $17/capita on health — less than 1/3 of WHO's $60 minimum
- Emergency dispatching systems exist in only ~83% of surveyed countries — with massive quality gaps in developing nations
- In most developing countries, emergency response is "largely ad hoc, uncoordinated, and inefficient" (PMC review)
