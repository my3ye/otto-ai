## Established Voice Rules

**Do not mix metaphors.** If a piece uses the "ladder" metaphor (SOS Systems), commit to it. If using the "river" metaphor (power/flow), commit to it. Never use both in one piece.

**SOS Systems = ladder metaphor.** The ladder does not ask where you came from. It meets you where you are. Consistent across all SOS/505 content.

**MY3YE / power / governance = river metaphor.** "Power is not a lake. Power is a river."

**Physics over ideology.** Never use the word "believe." Say "physics" or "mechanics" or "engineering." SOS Systems: "Not idealism. Engineering." MY3YE: "This is not punishment. This is physics."

**Open with a scene, not a thesis.** The "Ladder Out" article opens with someone in a displacement camp — not a statement about what SOS Systems does. Scene first, thesis emerges from it.

**Why:** The brand voice assumes the reader is intelligent. Stating the thesis first is condescending. Dropping into a scene earns the reader's trust before making the claim.

**How to apply:** Every Paragraph article should open with a concrete moment or image. Not "SOS Systems builds X" but "Somewhere right now, someone is..."

## Words That Work for SOS Systems
- arrive, begin, pathway, ladder, compound, earn, build, access, structure, contribute, rise
- "Not a shelter model — a pathway model"
- "The world does not have a talent shortage. It has an access shortage."

## Words to Avoid
- charity (unless explicitly being contrasted against "structure")
- emergency (use "crisis" — more universal)
- decentralized (jargon — use "no central chokepoint" or "no single actor can capture")
- revolutionary (banned per brand guide)

## Structural Decisions — Dream Beat Articles

**"A Game Where Your Victory Does Not Require My Defeat" — opening move.** Opens with zero-sum as felt reality ("the game you were born into was already running"), not as thesis to be argued against. The reader recognizes the game before the inversion lands. This is the pattern for the Dream beat: ground the old world first, then show the new one is already built.

**Canonical brand line as closing H2 thesis, not as opener.** The Dream brand line ("A game where your victory does not require my defeat") lands in the body of the final section — not as the section title, not as the opener. It arrives after the reader has been shown three concrete examples. Earned, not announced.

**"Not idealism. Engineering." — cross-article closing move.** Used in "Power Is Not a Lake" (engineering per Law beat) and now in the Dream beat article ("This Is Not Idealism" section). Works because the reader expects the Dream beat to be visionary/soft — the engineering landing is the subversion. Do not use this closing move for any other articles — it now has two instances, enough.

**River closes but does not drive the Dream piece.** The river metaphor closes ("The river moves. Move with it.") but the piece does not rest on the river as central image — that would duplicate "Power Is Not a Lake." The Dream article is structured around the game metaphor. River is the closing note, not the frame.

## Per-Project Dominant Metaphors (Inception Articles)

Each project article uses one committed metaphor — do not import any of these into another project's piece.

- **ONEON**: architecture / commons / weight — "structural property not feature," "node not user," "inscribed not hosted"
- **TUSITA**: dome / circle / architecture of belonging — the dome holds multiplicity without flattening it
- **KOINK**: physics / pressure / structure — "this is not punishment. this is physics." anti-whale as routing, not rule
- **SHAKRAH**: substrate / ground / nervous system — not optimization, restoration; "load-bearing part that looks soft"
- **OTTOLABS**: forge / workshop / hands / stage — "digital performance on an analog stage," workshop as honest expression
- **SOS SYSTEMS**: ladder — the ladder meets you where you are, does not ask where you came from
- **MY3YE / power**: river — "power is not a lake. power is a river." — MY3YE overview articles only

**Rule:** Never mix two of these metaphors in one piece. Pick one; commit fully.

## Structural Decisions — River Articles

**"Power Is Not a Lake" — opening move.** Do not open with the lake/river contrast as an abstract framing device. The article that owns the river metaphor fully opens viscerally — "A lake is full. That is its nature. It holds." — and builds to mechanism. The opening is a physical image, not a thesis statement.

**The priest as power structure.** "The Machine Needs No Priest" uses "the priest" and "the keeper" as interchangeable terms for institutional gatekeepers. Not a religious critique — a structural one. The keeper's power is always asymmetry: they have access, you do not. This framing does not require crypto-specific language. Write it as governance argument first; blockchain is one example.

**Do not import the river metaphor into the priest article.** Article 2 (the priest/machine piece) makes no river references. The governing image there is architecture: walls, structure, the machine itself. Keeps the metaphors cleanly separated across pieces.
