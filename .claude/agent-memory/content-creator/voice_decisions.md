## Established Voice Rules

**Do not mix metaphors.** If a piece uses the "ladder" metaphor (SOS Systems), commit to it. If using the "river" metaphor (power/flow), commit to it. Never use both in one piece.

**SOS Systems = ladder metaphor.** The ladder does not ask where you came from. It meets you where you are. Consistent across all SOS/505 content.

**MY3YE / power / governance = river metaphor.** "Power is not a lake. Power is a river."

**Physics over ideology.** Never use the word "believe." Say "physics" or "mechanics" or "engineering." SOS Systems: "Not idealism. Engineering." MY3YE: "This is not punishment. This is physics."

**Open with a scene, not a thesis.** The "Ladder Out" article opens with someone in a displacement camp — not a statement about what SOS Systems does. Scene first, thesis emerges from it.

**Why:** The brand voice assumes the reader is intelligent. Stating the thesis first is condescending. Dropping into a scene earns the reader's trust before making the claim.

**How to apply:** Every Paragraph article should open with a concrete moment or image. Not "SOS Systems builds X" but "Somewhere right now, someone is..."

## Words That Work for SOS Systems
- arrive, begin, pathway, ladder, compound, earn, build, access, structure, contribute, rise
- "Not a shelter model — a pathway model"
- "The world does not have a talent shortage. It has an access shortage."

## Words to Avoid
- charity (unless explicitly being contrasted against "structure")
- emergency (use "crisis" — more universal)
- decentralized (jargon — use "no central chokepoint" or "no single actor can capture")
- revolutionary (banned per brand guide)
